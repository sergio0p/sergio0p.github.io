The FOC linear system — is $A\,(f,g)^\top=(1,1)^\top$ correct?

Short answer: the matrix $A$ and the left-hand side are right, but the right-hand side is not $(1,1)$ — it is $(1/\varphi_L,\,1/\varphi_H)$.

Notation

$x=G_L,\ y=G_H$; densities $f:=g_L=x'(b)$, $g:=g_H=y'(b)$; types $\varphi_L=F_L^{-1}(x)$, $\varphi_H=F_H^{-1}(y)$.

The two FOCs

A type-$v$ bidder in group $i$ (linear cost, payoff $v\,W_i(b)-b$) has FOC $v\,W_i'(b)=1$; the type bidding $b$ is $v=\varphi_i$, so

$$\varphi_L\,W_L'=1,\qquad \varphi_H\,W_H'=1.$$

Why the $\varphi$'s land on the right

$A=(a_{ij})$ was defined from $W_i'$ without the $\varphi$'s: $\;W_L'=a_{11}f+a_{12}g,\ \ W_H'=a_{21}f+a_{22}g$. Substituting into $\varphi_iW_i'=1$ keeps $\varphi_i$ multiplying row $i$, so it appears as a reciprocal on the right:

$$A\begin{pmatrix}f\\[2pt] g\end{pmatrix}=\begin{pmatrix}1/\varphi_L\\[2pt] 1/\varphi_H\end{pmatrix}, \qquad A=\begin{pmatrix}a_{11}&a_{12}\\ a_{21}&a_{22}\end{pmatrix}=\frac{\partial(W_L,W_H)}{\partial(x,y)}.$$

Getting a $(1,1)$ right side — absorb the types

A $(1,1)$ right side is legitimate only if the types are folded into the matrix, i.e. row-scale $A$ by $\operatorname{diag}(\varphi_L,\varphi_H)$:

$$\underbrace{\begin{pmatrix}\varphi_L\,a_{11}&\varphi_L\,a_{12}\\[2pt]\varphi_H\,a_{21}&\varphi_H\,a_{22}\end{pmatrix}}_{\operatorname{diag}(\varphi_L,\varphi_H)\,A} \begin{pmatrix}f\\[2pt] g\end{pmatrix}=\begin{pmatrix}1\\[2pt] 1\end{pmatrix}.$$

The two forms are equivalent. Do not drop the $\varphi$'s: they carry all the type information and are exactly where the intimidation condition (e.g. $\varphi_H>3\varphi_L$ near the top) enters. So $A\,(f,g)^\top=(1,1)^\top$ is incorrect for the pure-Jacobian $A$; use $(1/\varphi_L,1/\varphi_H)$, or move the $\varphi$'s into $A$.
