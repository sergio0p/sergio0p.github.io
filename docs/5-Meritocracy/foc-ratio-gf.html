Solving for $g/f$ via the off-diagonal common term $z$

Continuing from foc-system-setup.html. The system is $A\,(f,g)^\top=(1/\varphi_L,1/\varphi_H)^\top$ with $x=G_L$, $y=G_H$, $f=g_L$, $g=g_H$. Assume the off-diagonals are proportional with common polynomial $z$:

$$a_{12}=\beta\,z,\qquad a_{21}=\alpha\,z,\qquad \frac{\beta}{\alpha}=\frac{a_{12}}{a_{21}}=\frac{h}{\ell}\ \ (\alpha,\beta\in\mathbb Q).$$

1. Eliminate the right-hand side

Both FOCs equal $1$, so set them equal — $\varphi_L W_L'=\varphi_H W_H'$. The right-hand sides (and $\det A$) cancel, leaving one homogeneous equation in $(f,g)$:

$$\varphi_L\,(a_{11}f+a_{12}g)=\varphi_H\,(a_{21}f+a_{22}g) \;\Longrightarrow\; f\,(\varphi_L a_{11}-\varphi_H a_{21})=g\,(\varphi_H a_{22}-\varphi_L a_{12}).$$

Hence the ratio, with no $\varphi$-reciprocals and no determinant:

$$\frac{g}{f}=\frac{\varphi_L\,a_{11}-\varphi_H\,a_{21}}{\varphi_H\,a_{22}-\varphi_L\,a_{12}}.$$

2. Insert the common term $z$

Substituting $a_{21}=\alpha z$, $a_{12}=\beta z$:

$$\frac{g}{f}=\frac{\varphi_L\,a_{11}-\alpha\,\varphi_H\,z}{\varphi_H\,a_{22}-\beta\,\varphi_L\,z}.$$

Reading it: $z$ enters only through the two cross terms — weighted by $\varphi_H$ (numerator) and $\varphi_L$ (denominator) — while the diagonals $a_{11}$ (the $\varphi_L$ term) and $a_{22}$ (the $\varphi_H$ term) sit untouched. Each line is "own-diagonal $\times$ own-$\varphi$ $-$ cross-$z$ $\times$ other-$\varphi$." Since $\beta=(h/\ell)\alpha$, the denominator's $z$-term is $(h/\ell)\,\alpha\,\varphi_L z$, so a single scalar $\alpha$ and the ratio $h/\ell$ carry all the off-diagonal content.

3. Instance — Draw 5 ($k=5,h=8,\ell=2$)

$$z=(y-1)^3y^3(2xy-x-y),\quad \alpha=70,\ \beta=280\ \big(\beta/\alpha=4=h/\ell\big),$$ $$a_{11}=70\,(y-1)^4y^4,\qquad a_{22}=35\,(y-1)^2y^2\big(14x^2y^2-14x^2y+3x^2-14xy^2+8xy+3y^2\big).$$

Numerically verified: the boxed $g/f$ formula matches the direct Cramer solve of $A(f,g)^\top=(1/\varphi_L,1/\varphi_H)^\top$ to machine precision (rtol $10^{-12}$), and $a_{12}/a_{21}=4=h/\ell$.
