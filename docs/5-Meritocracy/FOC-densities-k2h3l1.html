Density FOC system for $k=2,\ h=3,\ \ell=1$

Parreiras–Rubinchik (2010) linear-in-densities approach · all coefficients Symbolics-verified · residual = 0

One H-bidder faces $2$ other H's and the single L; the L-bidder faces all $3$ H's. With $k=2$ prizes a bidder wins iff at most one rival outbids him. Write $x=1-G_H,\ y=1-G_L$ for the tail probabilities and $g_i=G_i'(b)$ for the bid densities.

1. Interim win probabilities

$$W_H(b)=G_H^2+2\,G_H(1-G_H)\,G_L,\qquad W_L(b)=3G_H^2-2G_H^3 .$$

$W_H=\Pr(0\text{ of }3\text{ rivals above})+\Pr(\text{exactly }1)$, the rivals being $2$H$+1$L. $W_L$ depends on $G_H$ only — the lone L has no L-rival.

2. Differentiate in $b$

Chain rule, collecting the two densities:

$$W_H'(b)=2\big[G_H(1-G_L)+G_L(1-G_H)\big]\,g_H\;+\;2\,G_H(1-G_H)\,g_L,$$ $$W_L'(b)=6\,G_H(1-G_H)\,g_H .$$

Each coefficient is a marginal-pivotal probability: in $W_L'$, three H-rivals $\times\,g_H\times\Pr(\text{exactly one of the other two H above})=3g_H\cdot 2x(1-x)$. In $W_H'$ the $g_H$-coefficient is $2g_H\cdot[x(1-y)+(1-x)y]$ (one of $\{$other H, the L$\}$ above) and the $g_L$-coefficient is $g_L\cdot 2x(1-x)$ (one of two H above).

3. Stationarity ⇒ an affine system in $(g_H,g_L)$

A type $v$ in group $i$ maximizes $v\,W_i(b)-b$, so $W_i'(b)\,\varphi_i(b)=1$ with $\varphi_i(b)=F_i^{-1}(G_i(b))$ the type bidding $b$. The two FOCs are linear (affine) in $(g_H,g_L)$:

$$\begin{pmatrix} 2\big[G_H(1-G_L)+G_L(1-G_H)\big] & 2G_H(1-G_H)\\[4pt] 6G_H(1-G_H) & 0 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix}g_H\\[2pt] g_L\end{pmatrix} =\begin{pmatrix}1/\varphi_H\\[2pt] 1/\varphi_L\end{pmatrix}=:A\,\mathbf g=\mathbf c .$$

The matrix is lower-anti-triangular — the L-row has a zero in the $g_L$ slot (the L-FOC contains no $g_L$). Its determinant is the Jacobian double-zero already met in the boundary-layer analysis:

$$\det A \;=\; -\,2G_H(1-G_H)\cdot 6G_H(1-G_H)\;=\;-12\,G_H^2(1-G_H)^2 .$$

4. Solve (Cramer)

$$\boxed{\,g_H \;=\; \frac{1}{6\,G_H(1-G_H)\,\varphi_L}\,}$$ $$\boxed{\,g_L \;=\; \frac{1}{2\,G_H(1-G_H)\,\varphi_H}\;-\;\frac{G_H(1-G_L)+G_L(1-G_H)}{6\,G_H^{2}(1-G_H)^{2}\,\varphi_L}\,}$$

Equivalently, the triangular reading: the L-FOC alone pins $g_H=1/[6G_H(1-G_H)\varphi_L]$; substitute into the H-FOC and solve the remaining scalar equation for $g_L$. Substituting both back into $A\mathbf g$ returns $\mathbf c$ exactly (Symbolics residual $=[0,0]$).

5. What the solution shows

  • Density blow-up at the top. As $G_H\to1$ the common factor $G_H(1-G_H)\to0$, so $g_H\sim 1/[6\,\bar v_L\,(1-G_H)]\to\infty$ — the $k=2$ explosion that drives the boundary-layer common-top argument.
  • $g_L$ is a difference of two blow-ups. Both terms diverge like $(1-G_H)^{-1}$ and $(1-G_H)^{-2}$; finiteness of $g_L$ near the top is a cancellation condition, which is exactly the $0/0$ that forced introducing the rate $\rho=\lim(1-G_L)/(1-G_H)$.
  • Sign / admissibility. $g_H>0$ automatically (all factors positive on $0

6. The ratio $g_L/g_H$

Dividing the two boxed solutions and simplifying (Symbolics), the $\varphi_L$ and the $(1-G_H)$ powers cancel. The subtracted term then splits across its denominator into a tail ratio and a level ratio:

$$\frac{G_H(1-G_L)+G_L(1-G_H)}{G_H(1-G_H)}=\frac{1-G_L}{1-G_H}+\frac{G_L}{G_H}.$$

so the density ratio is just three terms:

$$\boxed{\;\frac{g_L}{g_H}\;=\;3\,\frac{\varphi_L}{\varphi_H}\;-\;\frac{1-G_L}{1-G_H}\;-\;\frac{G_L}{G_H}\;}$$

Symbolics: simplify(ratio − three-term form) = 0.

7. Ruling out the common top — drop the positive term

The middle term is a ratio of tails; nothing local pins its value, and at a common top it is the $0/0$ we cannot evaluate. But we never need to: below the top both tails are strictly positive, so $\dfrac{1-G_L}{1-G_H}>0$, and dropping a positive term only weakens an upper bound —

$$\frac{g_L}{g_H}\;<\;3\,\frac{\varphi_L}{\varphi_H}-\frac{G_L}{G_H}\qquad\text{for every }b<\bar b.$$

Let $b\to\bar b$. At a common top $G_H,G_L\to1$, so $\dfrac{G_L}{G_H}\to1$, while $\varphi_L\to\bar v_L,\ \varphi_H\to\bar v_H$. Hence

$$\limsup_{b\to\bar b}\frac{g_L}{g_H}\;\le\;3\,\frac{\bar v_L}{\bar v_H}-1.$$

Under intimidation $\bar v_H>3\bar v_L$ the right-hand side is strictly negative, so $g_L/g_H<0$ near the top; since $g_H>0$ this forces $g_L<0$ — impossible for a density. Therefore $\bar b_H=\bar b_L$ cannot occur.

Inputs used: (i) the exact three-term identity of §6; (ii) $G_L/G_H\to1$, which is just the definition of a common top; (iii) $\varphi_i\to\bar v_i$ at the top. Nothing else. In particular:

  • No assumption that $\dfrac{1-G_L}{1-G_H}$ converges, and no rate $\rho$ — the boundary-layer substitution $G_L=1-\rho\varepsilon$ silently assumed that tail ratio had a fixed limit; here the term's positivity is all that matters, so its rate is irrelevant.
  • No use of $G_L>G_H$ — only $G_L/G_H\to1$.
  • The pointwise "$<$" relaxes to "$\le$" under $\limsup$, but intimidation makes the bound strictly negative, so the contradiction survives.
