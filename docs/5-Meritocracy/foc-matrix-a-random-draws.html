Factoring the FOC matrix $A$ — random $(k,h,\ell)$ draws

Write $x=G_L$, $y=G_H$. The matrix in the density FOCs $A\,(g_L,g_H)^\top=(1/\varphi_L,1/\varphi_H)^\top$ is the Jacobian

$$A=\frac{\partial(W_L,W_H)}{\partial(x,y)} =\begin{pmatrix}\partial W_L/\partial x & \partial W_L/\partial y\\[4pt] \partial W_H/\partial x & \partial W_H/\partial y\end{pmatrix},$$

where $W_i$ is the interim win probability (win $=$ at most $k-1$ of the rivals bid above: own group minus one, plus the other group in full).

1. The generator (Julia / Nemo)

Draws $k\in\{2,\dots,5\}$, $h\in\{k{+}1,\dots,k{+}6\}$, $\ell\in\{1,\dots,k{+}6\}$; builds each $W_i$ over $\mathbb Q[x,y]$, differentiates, and factors each entry over $\mathbb Q$ (Random.seed!(13), so the five draws below are reproducible).

R,(x,y)=polynomial_ring(QQ,["x","y"])           # x=GL, y=GH
function winprob(k,h,l,group)
    rH = group==:H ? h-1 : h;  rL = group==:L ? l-1 : l
    W = zero(R)
    for a in 0:rH, b in 0:rL
        if a+b <= k-1
            W += binomial(rH,a)*binomial(rL,b)*(1-y)^a*y^(rH-a)*(1-x)^b*x^(rL-b)
        end
    end; W
end
matrixA(k,h,l) = (WL=winprob(k,h,l,:L); WH=winprob(k,h,l,:H);
    (derivative(WL,x),derivative(WL,y),derivative(WH,x),derivative(WH,y)))
# each entry then passed through Nemo factor()

3. What recurs

  • Off-diagonals are proportional. In every draw $a_{12}$ and $a_{21}$ carry the identical irreducible polynomial; only the rational prefactor differs. The ratio is $a_{12}/a_{21}=h/\ell$ throughout (Draws 1–5: $8/6,\ 6/8,\ 8/1,\ 8/6,\ 8/2$ — and $3/2$ in the earlier $h{=}3,\ell{=}2$ case). An empirical regularity here, not yet proved in general.
  • $\ell=1\Rightarrow a_{11}=0$ (Draw 3): the lone $L$ has no $L$-rival, so $W_L$ is free of $x$ and the system goes triangular.
  • Each entry $=$ (rational scalar)$\cdot x^{p}y^{q}\cdot$(irreducible factor). The monomial $x^{p}y^{q}$ records how many rivals of each pool sit "all below"; small $\ell$ thins the $x$-content and leaves high powers of $(y-1)=-(1-G_H)$ (Draws 3, 5).

Sign: factorization over $\mathbb Q$ is unique only up to $\pm$ units, so FLINT may carry a negative leading constant — e.g. $-20y^{7}x^{3}(3xy-2x-y)=20y^{7}x^{3}(2x+y-3xy)$. All entries Nemo/FLINT-factored (complete over $\mathbb Q$).
