Common top via sign-discarding — $k=2,\ h=3,\ \ell=2$

Companion to FOC-densities-k2h3l1.html. Here the algebra is NOT clean (full 2×2 FOC system), but discarding sign-definite terms still rules out $\bar b_H=\bar b_L$. All identities Symbolics-verified; the kill is numerically checked. $\lambda=k/h=2/3$, so intimidation's necessary condition is unchanged: $\bar v_H>3\bar v_L$.

1. Win probabilities

An H-bidder faces $2$H$+2$L; an L-bidder faces $3$H$+1$L. With $k=2$, win = at most one rival above:

$$W_H=G_H^2G_L^2+2(1-G_H)G_HG_L^2+2(1-G_L)G_LG_H^2,$$ $$W_L=G_H^3G_L+3(1-G_H)G_H^2G_L+G_H^3(1-G_L).$$

Unlike $\ell=1$, $W_L$ now depends on $G_L$ — the lone-L simplification is gone, so the FOC system below is a genuine $2\times2$.

2. The affine FOC system

$W_i'(b)=a_{iH}\,g_H+a_{iL}\,g_L$ with $a_{ij}=\partial W_i/\partial G_j$; stationarity $W_i'\varphi_i=1$ gives $A\,(g_H,g_L)^\top=(1/\varphi_H,1/\varphi_L)^\top$. The coefficients factor through two polynomials

$$P:=G_H+2G_L-3G_HG_L,\qquad Q:=2G_H+G_L-3G_HG_L,$$ $$a_{HH}=2G_LQ,\quad a_{HL}=2G_HP,\quad a_{LH}=3G_HP,\quad a_{LL}=3G_H^2(1-G_H).$$

so $a_{LH}=\tfrac32 a_{HL}$. Cramer's rule gives both densities, and the object we sign is their ratio (as in the $\ell=1$ file):

$$\frac{g_L}{g_H}=\frac{N}{D},\qquad N:=a_{HH}\varphi_H-a_{LH}\varphi_L,\qquad D:=a_{LL}\varphi_L-a_{HL}\varphi_H,$$ $$\det A=a_{HH}a_{LL}-a_{HL}a_{LH}.$$

($N,D$ are the Cramer numerators of $g_L,g_H$ cleared of the common $1/(\varphi_H\varphi_L\det A)$, so the ratio is literally $N/D$.)

3. Two sign facts near the common top

Write $t=1-G_H,\ s=1-G_L$; at a common top both $\to0$. Expanding (Symbolics):

$$2G_LQ-G_HP = 3s+2t^2-4s^2-4st+\dots \;>\;0,\qquad G_H(1-G_H)-2P = -(3t+2s)+O(\text{quad}) \;<\;0.$$

Both hold on a full left-neighborhood of $\bar b$ for every approach ($s,t\ge0$ small, no relative-rate restriction): the linear terms dominate the remainders. Also $a_{HL}=2G_HP>0$, $2G_LQ>0$ and $\varphi_i>0$ there. (No determinant is needed — see §4.)

4. The kill — a sign clash forced by both densities being nonnegative

The two FOCs, combined to eliminate the right-hand sides, give an exact relation between the densities (Symbolics residual $=0$; $\det A$ cancels and never appears):

$$g_H\,N - g_L\,D \;=\; \varphi_H\underbrace{(a_{HH}g_H+a_{HL}g_L)}_{=\,1/\varphi_H}-\varphi_L\underbrace{(a_{LH}g_H+a_{LL}g_L)}_{=\,1/\varphi_L}\;=\;1-1\;=\;0 \quad\Longrightarrow\quad g_H\,N=g_L\,D.$$

Now $N$ and $D$ each split, by an identity, into an intimidation-surplus piece $(\varphi_H-3\varphi_L)$ and a purely polynomial piece:

$$N \;=\; \underbrace{(\varphi_H-3\varphi_L)\,2G_LQ}_{\ge0}\;+\;\underbrace{3\varphi_L\,(2G_LQ-G_HP)}_{\ge0}\;>\;0,$$ $$D \;=\; \underbrace{3G_H\varphi_L\big(G_H(1-G_H)-2P\big)}_{\le0}\;-\;\underbrace{(\varphi_H-3\varphi_L)\,2G_HP}_{\ge0}\;<\;0,$$

both near the top: $\varphi_H\to\bar v_H,\varphi_L\to\bar v_L$ with $\bar v_H>3\bar v_L$ give $\varphi_H-3\varphi_L>0$, and §3 gives the two polynomial signs. So $N>0$ and $D<0$ on a left-neighborhood of $\bar b$.

Feed this into $g_HN=g_LD$:

$$\underbrace{g_H}_{\ge0}\underbrace{N}_{>0}\;=\;\underbrace{g_L}_{\ge0}\underbrace{D}_{<0} \;\Longrightarrow\; (\ge0)=(\le0)\;\Longrightarrow\; g_H=g_L=0 .$$

Both densities are nonnegative (they are densities), so the left side is $\ge0$ and the right side is $\le0$ — the only escape is $g_H=g_L=0$ throughout a left-neighborhood of $\bar b$. But then $G_H,G_L$ are constant there and cannot rise to $1$ at $\bar b$: contradiction. So the common top $\bar b_H=\bar b_L$ cannot occur. $\blacksquare$

Why this is cleaner than identifying a negative density. We never need $\det A$, never need to know which density would go negative, and never need $g_H>0$ as a separate input. Both densities are nonnegative by definition; the exact relation $g_HN=g_LD$ with $N>0,D<0$ is already incompatible with that — a positive multiple of $g_H$ equal to a negative multiple of $g_L$. Equivalently $g_L/g_H=N/D<0$, impossible for two nonnegative numbers. (In the $\ell=1$ file the same logic applies; there the triangular system happened to hand us $g_H>0$ explicitly, which is why the asymmetry went unnoticed.)

5. What this buys (vs. the $\ell=1$ argument and the old $\rho$ slicing)

  • Rate-free. Nothing assumes $(1-G_L)/(1-G_H)$ converges. The two surpluses are sign-definite for all small $s,t\ge0$; the relative rate never enters.
  • Pointwise, not just $\limsup$. The contradiction $g_L<0$ holds at every $b$ in a left-neighborhood of $\bar b$ — stronger than the $\ell=1$ $\limsup$ statement.
  • The method, not the formula, generalizes. The clean three-term identity of $\ell=1$ is gone; what survives is: write the signed density's numerator, split off (I) the intimidation surplus $(\varphi_H-3\varphi_L)\times(\text{positive coeff})$ and (II) a purely polynomial surplus, and read both signs. The only model-specific labor is checking the two polynomial facts of §3.
  • Intimidation enters exactly once, through $\varphi_H>3\varphi_L$ near the top — i.e. the necessary condition $(1-\lambda)\bar v_H>\bar v_L$ at $\lambda=2/3$. Drop it and term (I) can flip; that is the boundary the result lives on.
