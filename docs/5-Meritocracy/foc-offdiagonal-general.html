Off-diagonal entries in general: common term $z$ and the constant $h/\ell$

Notation $x=G_L$, $y=G_H$. The off-diagonals are $a_{12}=\partial W_L/\partial y$ and $a_{21}=\partial W_H/\partial x$. Both equal the same polynomial $z$ times a group size:

$$a_{12}=h\,z,\qquad a_{21}=\ell\,z,\qquad \boxed{\dfrac{a_{12}}{a_{21}}=\dfrac{h}{\ell}}$$ $$\boxed{\,z(k,h,\ell;x,y)=\!\!\sum_{a=\max(0,\,k-\ell)}^{\min(h-1,\,k-1)}\!\!\binom{h-1}{a}\binom{\ell-1}{k-1-a}\,(1-y)^{a}\,y^{\,h-1-a}\,(1-x)^{\,k-1-a}\,x^{\,\ell-k+a}\,}$$

So the proportionality constant is $h/\ell$ — depending on the group sizes alone, free of $k,x,y$ — and the common term $z$ has a clean reading:

$$z=\Pr\!\big[\text{exactly }k-1\text{ of the reduced rival pool }(h-1)\text{ H}+(\ell-1)\text{ L are above}\big].$$

$z$ is the pivotal probability of the once-smaller pool (drop one rival from each group): $\binom{h-1}{a}(1-y)^a y^{h-1-a}$ is "exactly $a$ of $h-1$ H-rivals above," $\binom{\ell-1}{k-1-a}(1-x)^{k-1-a}x^{\ell-1-(k-1-a)}$ is "exactly $k-1-a$ of $\ell-1$ L-rivals above," summed over the split that totals $k-1$. It is the same boundary object that drives the $\widehat W^{(k-1)}_{h-1,\ell-1}$ recursion.

Derivation

One identity does it. For a binomial CDF in its success probability $q$,

$$\frac{d}{dq}\Pr\!\big[\mathrm{Bin}(\ell,q)\le m\big]=-\ell\binom{\ell-1}{m}q^{m}(1-q)^{\ell-1-m}.$$

An H-bidder wins iff $N_H+N_L\le k-1$ with $N_H\sim\mathrm{Bin}(h-1,1-y)$ and $N_L\sim\mathrm{Bin}(\ell,1-x)$ independent. Here $x$ enters only through $N_L$ via $q=1-x$, so condition on $N_H=a$, apply the identity at $m=k-1-a$, and use $\partial/\partial x=-\partial/\partial q$:

$$a_{21}=\frac{\partial W_H}{\partial x} =\ell\sum_{a}\underbrace{\binom{h-1}{a}(1-y)^a y^{h-1-a}}_{\Pr[N_H=a]} \binom{\ell-1}{k-1-a}(1-x)^{k-1-a}x^{\ell-k+a}=\ell\,z.$$

For $a_{12}$, the tagged L-bidder faces $h$ H-rivals and $\ell-1$ L-rivals; $y$ enters only through $N_H\sim\mathrm{Bin}(h,1-y)$, so the identity now pulls out the factor $h$ and a $\binom{h-1}{\,\cdot\,}$. Reindexing $a\leftrightarrow k-1-b$ shows the leftover sum is the identical $z$:

$$a_{12}=\frac{\partial W_L}{\partial y}=h\,z\quad\Longrightarrow\quad \frac{a_{12}}{a_{21}}=\frac{h}{\ell}.$$

Relation to the artifacts' $(\alpha,\beta,z_{\text{art}})$

Taking the common term to be this $z$, the proportionality scalars are simply $\beta=h,\ \alpha=\ell$. The fully-factored $z_{\text{art}}$ and integer scalars printed by Nemo just pull a further constant $c$ out of $z$ (so $z=c\,z_{\text{art}}$, $\alpha=\ell c$, $\beta=hc$). E.g. Draw 5 $(k{=}5,h{=}8,\ell{=}2)$: $z=35\,(y-1)^3y^3(x+y-2xy)$, $c=35$, $(\alpha,\beta)=(70,280)$.

Symbolically verified ($\partial W_L/\partial y-h\,z=0$ and $\partial W_H/\partial x-\ell\,z=0$, residual $0$ over $\mathbb Q$) for $(k,h,\ell)\in\{(2,3,2),(2,8,6),(3,8,6),(5,8,2),(2,6,8),(3,8,1),(4,6,5),(3,5,4),(5,9,7),(4,10,1),(2,4,3)\}$.
