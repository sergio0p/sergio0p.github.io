The $W\!\cdot\!G$ difference recursion for heterogeneous players

4-WinProb · arbitrary distinct players · $k$ arbitrary · Symbolics residual 0 (all pairs, $n,k$ tested) · 2026-06-13

The Meritocracy project found that for two groups $H,L$ the products $W_iG_i$ satisfy a one-step recursion, $W_LG_L-W_HG_H=(G_L-G_H)\widehat W^{(k-1)}_{h-1,\ell-1}$. With distinct odds ratios already the native object here, that identity is not just transferable — it is cleaner, because in WinProb the products $W_iG_i$ share a common denominator. This note states and derives the heterogeneous version and locates it against the recovery problem.

1. The dictionary

WinProb's odds ratio is the Meritocracy "odds of being above." Player $\ell$ is above (beats the in-the-money threshold) with odds $r_\ell>0$, equivalently below with probability

$$r_\ell=\frac{1-G_\ell}{G_\ell},\qquad G_\ell=\frac{1}{1+r_\ell},\qquad 1-G_\ell=\frac{r_\ell}{1+r_\ell}.$$

With $k$ prizes, player $i$ wins iff at most $k-1$ of the others are above. In the global-polynomial form of odds_ratio_global_polynomial.html, writing $g(t)=\prod_\ell(1+r_\ell t)$ and $h_i(t)=g(t)/(1+r_i t)=\sum_m e_m(r_{-i})t^m$,

$$W_i=\frac{N_i}{D_i},\qquad N_i=\sum_{m=0}^{k-1}e_m(r_{-i}),\qquad D_i=h_i(1)=\prod_{\ell\neq i}(1+r_\ell)=\frac{g(1)}{1+r_i}.$$

2. The crux: $W_iG_i$ has a common denominator

Since $1/D_i=\prod_{\ell\neq i}(1+r_\ell)^{-1}=\prod_{\ell\neq i}G_\ell$, multiplying $W_i$ by its own CDF $G_i$ closes the product over all players:

$$W_iG_i=G_i\,N_i\!\!\prod_{\ell\neq i}G_\ell=N_i\prod_{\ell=1}^{n}G_\ell=\omega\,N_i,\qquad \omega:=\prod_{\ell=1}^{n}G_\ell=\frac{1}{g(1)}.$$

The weight $\omega=1/g(1)$ is the same for every $i$ — the per-$i$ denominator $D_i$ that obstructs everything else has been absorbed. So $W_iG_i$ is, up to the universal scalar $\omega$, simply the deletion numerator $N_i=\sum_{m=0}^{k-1}e_m(r_{-i})$. Differences are immediate:

$$W_iG_i-W_jG_j=\omega\,(N_i-N_j)=\frac{N_i-N_j}{g(1)}.$$

3. The numerator gap is rank one

The only work left is $N_i-N_j$. Split each $e_m$ on whether the other deleted index is taken. For any $m$, partitioning the monomials of $e_m(r_{-i})$ by whether they contain $r_j$,

$$e_m(r_{-i})=e_m(r_{-ij})+r_j\,e_{m-1}(r_{-ij}),\qquad e_m(r_{-j})=e_m(r_{-ij})+r_i\,e_{m-1}(r_{-ij}),$$

where $r_{-ij}$ deletes both indices. Subtracting kills the common $e_m(r_{-ij})$:

$$e_m(r_{-i})-e_m(r_{-j})=(r_j-r_i)\,e_{m-1}(r_{-ij}).$$

This is the pairwise companion of the single-deletion formula $e_m(r_{-i})=\sum_{s}(-r_i)^s E_{m-s}$ already in the odds-ratio note. Summing over the truncation $m=0,\dots,k-1$ telescopes the index by one:

$$N_i-N_j=\sum_{m=0}^{k-1}(r_j-r_i)\,e_{m-1}(r_{-ij})=(r_j-r_i)\sum_{m=0}^{k-2}e_m(r_{-ij})=(r_j-r_i)\,\widehat N_{ij},$$

where $\widehat N_{ij}:=\sum_{m=0}^{k-2}e_m(r_{-ij})$ is the numerator of the once-smaller contest: $k-1$ prizes among the $n-2$ players with both $i$ and $j$ removed.

4. The identity

Put the two factors of $\omega$ on $i,j$ and convert. With $\widehat W^{(k-1)}_{ij}:=\widehat N_{ij}\prod_{\ell\neq i,j}G_\ell$ the win probability of that deleted contest, $\omega=G_iG_j\prod_{\ell\neq i,j}G_\ell$ gives $\omega\,\widehat N_{ij}=G_iG_j\,\widehat W^{(k-1)}_{ij}$, and $(r_j-r_i)G_iG_j=G_i-G_j$ (clear denominators in $r=\tfrac{1-G}{G}$). Hence

$$\boxed{\;W_iG_i-W_jG_j=(G_i-G_j)\,\widehat W^{(k-1)}_{ij}\;}$$

— verbatim the Meritocracy recursion, now for arbitrary heterogeneous players. The gap between two $W\!\cdot\!G$ products equals the CDF gap times a win probability one prize and two players smaller. Equivalently, in pure global-polynomial form,

$$W_iG_i-W_jG_j=\frac{(r_j-r_i)}{g(1)}\,\widehat N_{ij}.$$

5. Specializations and sign

  • Two groups (Meritocracy). Collapse all H-players to a common $G_H$ and all L to $G_L$. Deleting one H and one L leaves the $(k-1,h-1,\ell-1)$ contest, so $\widehat W^{(k-1)}_{ij}=\widehat W^{(k-1)}_{h-1,\ell-1}$ and $G_i-G_j=G_L-G_H$ — the original boxed result, recovered as the homogeneous case.
  • $k=1$. $\widehat N_{ij}$ is an empty sum $=0$, so $W_iG_i=W_jG_j=\omega$. With one prize $W_i=\prod_{\ell\neq i}G_\ell=\omega/G_i$, and the symmetric product $W_iG_i=\omega$ is common to all — the WinProb image of $W_HG_H=W_LG_L$.
  • $k=2$. $\widehat N_{ij}=e_0=1$, so $\widehat W^{(1)}_{ij}=\prod_{\ell\neq i,j}G_\ell$ ("everyone else below") and $$W_iG_i-W_jG_j=(G_i-G_j)\!\!\prod_{\ell\neq i,j}\!\!G_\ell.$$
  • Monotone ordering. $\widehat W^{(k-1)}_{ij}>0$ on the interior, so $\operatorname{sign}(W_iG_i-W_jG_j)=\operatorname{sign}(G_i-G_j)=\operatorname{sign}(r_j-r_i)$ for all $k$. The map $i\mapsto W_iG_i$ is comonotone with $i\mapsto G_i$: order the products by the CDFs and the ordering never depends on $k$.

6. Where it bites on recovery

The inversion $\{W_i\}\mapsto\{r_i\}$ now has both halves of its linear skeleton, with all nonlinearity quarantined into one scalar factor and one recursive object:

aggregate (odds-ratio note):   $\displaystyle\sum_i N_i=\sum_{m=0}^{k-1}(n-m)E_m$

pairwise (this note):   $\displaystyle N_i-N_j=(r_j-r_i)\,\widehat N_{ij}$

Two readings of why this is a lever:

  • The filtration in $k$ is now explicit. The odds-ratio note observed that $W^{(k)}\to W^{(k+1)}$ appends the layer $e_k(r_{-i})$ to each numerator. The pairwise identity exhibits the matching downward step: level-$k$ numerator differences are level-$(k-1)$ deleted numerators, $N^{(k)}_i-N^{(k)}_j=(r_j-r_i)\widehat N^{(k-1)}_{ij}$. The sandwich/induction-in-$k$ becomes a literal recursion on $(k,\text{players})$, the WinProb analog of the Meritocracy GAP-GEN route.
  • It explains why $k=2$ closed on two scalars. For $k=2$, $\widehat N_{ij}=1$, so $N_i-N_j=r_j-r_i$: the numerator map is affine in the odds. That is exactly why recovery_k2_n4.html reduced to a fixed quadratic family $a_i^2-(A-2)a_i+W_iG=0$ — affine differences plus a single product constraint. For general $k$ the differences stay rank one but pick up the weight $\widehat N_{ij}$, which is the genuine obstruction the induction must carry.
Honest status. $W_iG_i$ is a function of the unknown odds, not raw data ($G_i$ is unknown); the identity is a structural relation among the unknowns, not a closed-form data constraint. Its value is that it makes the $k$-recursion exact and isolates the nonlinearity — the same posture as the Meritocracy note, where the analogous identities are exact but turning them into a full inversion proof for all $n$ is still open. This is the lever for the current Next: (push the $k=2$ inversion to general $n$), not a completed proof of it.

7. Symbolic verification

Win probabilities built from the exact "$\le k-1$ above" deletion expansion, $G_\ell=1/(1+r_\ell)$. For every pair $(i,j)$ the residual

$$W_iG_i-W_jG_j-(G_i-G_j)\,\widehat W^{(k-1)}_{ij}$$

simplifies to identically $0$ (Symbolics, simplify∘expand over $\mathbb{Q}(r)$) for $(n,k)\in\{(4,2),(5,2),(5,3),(6,3)\}$ — all pairs, no residual — and to machine zero ($\sim10^{-16}$) on random positive draws for $(n,k)\in\{(4,2),(5,2),(5,3),(6,3),(6,4),(7,4)\}$.
