Tropical cell structure of the Round-2 payoff $\Pi_2(v;(p,q))$

Two-bidder all-pay finale of the elimination contest — how the discrete-type equilibrium decomposes into a tropical (min-plus) cell complex carrying a classical affine map on each cell. All forms below are machine-verified (exact rational arithmetic): the composed solver matches the direct solver with 0 mismatches.

1. The global object

Each finalist arrives at Round 2 carrying a believed type-distribution: player $A$ a vector $p=(p_0,p_1,p_2)$ over values $(v_L,v_M,v_H)=(1,2,3)$, player $B$ a vector $q=(q_0,q_1,q_2)$. The interim payoff of $A$'s type $v$ is a function $\Pi_2(v;(p,q))$ of the pair of reputations. It is convenient to use the reputation tails

$$A_2=p_1+p_2,\quad A_3=p_2,\qquad B_2=q_1+q_2,\quad B_3=q_2 .$$

The headline structure:

$\Pi_2(v;\cdot)$ is piecewise-affine over a polyhedral subdivision of reputation space. The subdivision (which cell you are in) is decided by a min-plus computation; inside each cell the payoff is an ordinary affine function. It is not a single tropical polynomial — there is no global $\min$/$\max$ collapse (see §6).

2. The two layers

The whole equilibrium is built by an interval construction that sweeps the bid axis from the top down, peeling off one segment at a time. On a segment where $A$'s active value is $a$ and $B$'s is $c$, the two finalists deplete the probability mass of their current type at rates fixed by mutual indifference; the segment ends when one of them runs out. That single decision — who runs out first — is the only nonlinearity in the entire algorithm.

Layer 1 · tropical

Which cell — the regime word

The race between the two depletions is

$$L=\min\big(r_A\!\cdot\! c,\; r_B\!\cdot\! a\big) =\big(r_A\!\otimes\! c\big)\oplus\big(r_B\!\otimes\! a\big),$$

in the min-plus semiring $(\oplus,\otimes)=(\min,+)$ on log-coordinates, values entering as additive tropical constants $\log v$. Recording the argmin at each segment (:A, :B, or a tie :AB) yields a word — and the word is the cell.

Layer 2 · classical

What the payoff is — affine replay

With the word fixed, every $\min$ is resolved, so the construction is a deterministic diag(1/v)-weighted linear pipeline. Each type's payoff comes out as one affine form

$$\Pi_2(v_k)=v_k\,H_B(b^{\text{lo}}_k)-b^{\text{lo}}_k,$$

an affine function of $(p,q)$ on the cell. The $\tfrac13,\tfrac16,\dots$ coefficients are exactly the classical $1/v$ weights — they are not tropical.

Maslov-dequantization footnote: the $\;T\!\to\!0\;$ limit of the softmax / entropic best response, $\,T\log\sum_b e^{U/T}\to\max_b U$, is this max-plus limit — the trembles and temperature in the deep-learning solver are the dequantization parameter.

3. Warm-up: two types ($v_L=1,\,v_H=3$)

With only two types each finalist's reputation is a single scalar — $\mu=p_2$ (chance $A$ is High), $\nu=q_2$ (chance $B$ is High). Reputation space is the unit square, and there is exactly one wall, the diagonal $\mu=\nu$, splitting it into two cells:

Gluing the two affine pieces across the wall reproduces the familiar closed forms

$$U_A(\text{High})=2\big(1-\min(\mu,\nu)\big),\qquad U_A(\text{Low})=\tfrac23(\mu-\nu)^{+}.$$

Here the single scalar $\min(\mu,\nu)$ is the whole tropical content: the payoff is a genuine tropical polynomial of degree 1. This clean collapse is special to two types — it does not survive.

6. Why it is not one tropical polynomial

Each cell's affine form is exact inside its own cell but overshoots outside it. Taking the global $\max$ of all ten forms therefore fails ~99% of random points — the function is neither globally convex nor concave. (An earlier "$U_A(v_H)=\max$ of 8 forms" reading was a sampling artifact of the generic interior; the two corner cells 9–10 break it.) The honest object is exactly the two-layer picture:

tropical cell complex (Layer 1 — min-plus, picks 1 of 10 cells)  $\;\circ\;$  classical affine map (Layer 2 — one linear form per cell).  No global envelope.

8. Takeaway for the contest

The binary $\min(\mu,\nu)$ collapse is a degree-1 accident. With three types the Round-2 payoff carries ten affine pieces over a tropical subdivision of a 4-dimensional reputation space. For the Round-1 fixed point the number that matters — how many pieces of $\Pi_2$ the belief map can visit — is 10, and it is pure Layer-1 combinatorics, computable with no bid-space arithmetic.

Verification: composed solver (Layer 1 $\circ$ Layer 2) vs. direct interval-construction solver — 0 mismatches on bbar, both atoms, and all of $U_A,U_B$ over 6000 random $(p,q)$; own-cell affine form vs. exact $U_A(v_H)$ — 0 mismatches over 40000 points; exact rational arithmetic.
