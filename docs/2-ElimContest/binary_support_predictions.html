Does the belief non-linearity break piecewise-uniformity in the binary Round 1?

Companion to docs/turocy_support_analysis.html and Python/binary_predictions.py. 2-ElimContest · binary case, index 0 = High $v_H$, index 1 = Low $v_L$. Question (yours): the continuation value enters Round 1 through the belief $\mu$, non-linearly — doesn't that kill the piecewise-uniform structure Turocy guarantees?

Answer in one line. Yes — but in a located way you can test. Uniformity survives on intervals where only one type is active (belief is locally constant there), and is generically destroyed on intervals where both types co-mix (the belief moves with the bid). The break is governed by an explicit ODE, and "co-mixing at constant belief" is a knife-edge that maps exactly onto Turocy's condition for a non-singleton admissible active set. So the dynamic model is strictly richer than Turocy, not just broken.

1. The reduction (so every claim below is checkable)

A type $v$ choosing Round-1 bid $b$ in its pair earns

$$ U(v,b) \;=\; G(b)\,\tilde W\!\big(v,\mu(b)\big)\;-\;b, $$

with three objects, each computable from the solver output:

  • $G(b)=F(b)$ — the rival's Round-1 bid CDF. By symmetry it is the population CDF, so $G'(b)=g(b)$, the prior-weighted density $g=\tfrac12 f_L+\tfrac12 f_H$.
  • $\mu(b)=\dfrac{\tfrac12 f_H(b)}{\tfrac12 f_H(b)+\tfrac12 f_L(b)}=\dfrac{f_H(b)}{f_H(b)+f_L(b)}$ — the posterior that the bidder is High, induced by the on-path bid $b$.
  • $\tilde W(v,\mu)$ — the Round-2 continuation value of advancing as type $v$ when the finalist opponent holds belief $\mu$ about you. Crucially, $b$ enters $\tilde W$ only through $\mu(b)$: the bid is sunk and payoff-irrelevant in Round 2 except as a signal of type. This is the non-linearity you flagged.

2. The indifference condition and the dichotomy

If type $v$ randomizes (is active) over an interval, $U(v,b)$ is constant there, so $\frac{d}{db}U(v,b)=0$:

$$ \boxed{\;G'(b)\,\tilde W(v,\mu) \;+\; G(b)\,\tilde W_\mu(v,\mu)\,\mu'(b)\;=\;1\;}\tag{$\star$}$$

Compare Turocy: there the value of winning is a constant $V$, so $\tilde W_\mu\equiv 0$, the second term vanishes, and ($\star$) collapses to $G'(b)\,V=1$ — i.e. $G'$ constant, density uniform. The whole piecewise-uniform theorem is exactly this collapse. The extra term $G\,\tilde W_\mu\,\mu'$ is the dynamic belief channel.

SURVIVES Single-type intervals stay uniform

On an interval where only type $v$ is active, the on-path belief is degenerate and constant ($\mu\equiv 0$ on a Low-only interval, $\mu\equiv 1$ on a High-only interval). Then $\mu'=0$, the belief term drops, and ($\star$) gives

$$ G'(b)=g(b)=\frac{1}{\tilde W(v,\mu_{\text{const}})}\;=\;\text{constant}\quad\Longrightarrow\quad\text{uniform density.}$$

The level is even pinned: the High-only tail has $g=1/\tilde W(v_H,1)$, the Low-only stretch $g=1/\tilde W(v_L,0)$. So the belief non-linearity does nothing here — these regions are genuinely piecewise-uniform.

BREAKS Co-mixing intervals are generically non-uniform

On an interval where both types are active, ($\star$) holds for $v_L$ and $v_H$ simultaneously — two linear equations in the two unknowns $G'$ and $G\mu'$:

$$\begin{pmatrix}\tilde W_L & \tilde W_L'\\[2pt]\tilde W_H & \tilde W_H'\end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix}G'\\[2pt]G\mu'\end{pmatrix}=\begin{pmatrix}1\\1\end{pmatrix}, \qquad \tilde W_v\equiv\tilde W(v,\mu),\ \ \tilde W_v'\equiv\partial_\mu\tilde W(v,\mu).$$

By Cramer's rule (write $D=\tilde W_L\tilde W_H'-\tilde W_H\tilde W_L'$):

$$ G'(b)=\frac{\tilde W_H'(\mu)-\tilde W_L'(\mu)}{D(\mu)},\qquad G(b)\,\mu'(b)=\frac{\tilde W_L(\mu)-\tilde W_H(\mu)}{D(\mu)}.\tag{$\dagger$}$$

$G'$ is now a function of $\mu$ alone. Since $\mu$ moves across a co-mixing interval (that is what makes it co-mixing — the density ratio $f_H/f_L$ changes), $G'=g$ varies: the density is curved, not flat. And from ($\dagger$) you can reconstruct both type densities on the interval:

$$ f_H(b)=2\,G'(b)\,\mu(b),\qquad f_L(b)=2\,G'(b)\,\big(1-\mu(b)\big).$$

The belief direction is also pinned: $\operatorname{sign}\mu'=\operatorname{sign}\big[(\tilde W_L-\tilde W_H)/D\big]$ — a comparative static you can check.

The exact Turocy correspondence. $G'$ is constant on a co-mixing interval (uniformity restored) iff $\mu$ is constant there, which by ($\dagger$) requires $\tilde W_L(\mu^\ast)=\tilde W_H(\mu^\ast)$ — the two types value Round 2 equally at that belief. This is a knife-edge in $\mu$, and it is the same condition as Turocy's: a non-singleton active set is admissible only when the value vectors lie on a common hyperplane (his $\psi_{\cdot|k}$ rows linearly dependent). When that coincidence fails — the generic case — Turocy forbids co-mixing on an interval altogether, whereas here the dynamic term $\tilde W_\mu\neq0$ permits it but forces a moving belief and non-uniform density. Your intuition is right, and this is precisely the extra structure the two-stage game buys.

3. The three predictions, graded

PredictionVerdict under the belief non-linearity
Bottom-of-support = 0 ROBUST Independent of $\tilde W$. If the lowest bid $b_0>0$ in the union of supports, the bidder there wins with probability $G(b_0)=0$, pays $b_0$, and gets $0\cdot\tilde W-b_0<0$ — strictly worse than bidding $0$. So $b_0=0$ regardless of the continuation value. May appear as an atom at 0 (a type conceding Round 1).
Piecewise-uniform REFINED Holds only on single-type intervals; on overlap intervals it is replaced by the explicit non-uniform law ($\dagger$). The correct falsifiable statement is the regime-dependent one, not global uniformity.
$\le 2$ disjoint intervals / type DOWNGRADED to conjecture Turocy's $|\mathcal X_k|+1$ bound rests on the static linear $\Gamma$-graph, which the bid-dependent $\tilde W$ dismantles. Economically one still expects few regimes (a "hide" interval + a "bluff" interval), so $\le 2$ is a reasonable prior — but it is now a flag to test, not a theorem. $\ge 3$ components is a real possibility of the dynamic model.

The computed density instead slopes (CV $0.49$). Worth a refinement pass with lower temperature / tighter indifference weight on that tail.

Net. You now have something firmer than intuition: (1) a proof that uniformity must break on the overlap and survive on single-type regions, with the explicit density law ($\dagger$); (2) a knife-edge that ties the break precisely to Turocy's admissibility condition; and (3) a runnable checker that already confirms the overlap break and the bottom-at-0 property, while flagging a non-flat high tail that the current solution should not have if it were the exact equilibrium.

5. The ($\dagger$) test — from "is it curved?" to "does it match?"

Python/check_dagger.py upgrades the overlap check to the quantitative law. It reconstructs $\tilde W(v,\mu)$ by calling the same Round-2 solver (posteriors_to_payoffs) used in continuation_value.py, but feeding an arbitrary "me" belief $(\mu,1-\mu)$ rather than the belief the bidder's own bid induces — legitimate because $V(v,b)=\tilde W(v,\mu(b))$. It then compares, on the empirical overlap, the population density $g(b)$ to the closed form $G'(\mu)=(\tilde W_H'-\tilde W_L')/D$ and the belief drift $G\mu'$ to $(\tilde W_L-\tilde W_H)/D$.

The computed profile obeys the qualitative law but misses the level by a stable factor — it is close to, but not exactly, the ($\dagger$) co-mixing equilibrium, consistent with the residual exploitability gap.
  • Edge divergence is informative. Near $\mu\to0$ the predicted $G'$ blows up ($\approx49$ at M200): clean two-type co-mixing would need an infeasibly large density there, signalling that the bottom of the overlap is governed by the low-type atom/bluff structure rather than smooth co-mixing. The ~0.57 factor is stable in the interior $\mu\in[0.4,0.85]$.
Net of the upgrade. ($\dagger$) is the correct shape generator for the overlap — mechanism validated against the actual Round-2 solver — while the current solution sits a fixed, grid-robust ~40% below the indifference-exact density. That is a concrete quality target for the solver, sharper and more structural than the level-based Gap.
