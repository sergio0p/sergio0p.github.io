Round-1 Support Structure

Why IPV gives simple supports — the monotone posterior, the mirror symmetry, and the signaling obstruction

Pedagogical companion to round1_support_structure_handoff.md. 4-player, 2-round all-pay elimination contest; types v ∈ {L,M,H} = {1,2,3}, uniform prior.

Round 1 looks like an ordinary all-pay contest, but it hides a twist: the prize you win for advancing depends on how you bid, because your bid is a signal of your type. This note explains what that twist does to the bid supports — why the private-values (IPV) structure makes them simple single intervals (unlike Turocy's multi-interval supports), where the analysis gets stuck, and how that obstruction is finally resolved (§6): the continuation is $\sigma$-invariant in three types, and the equilibrium curve is closed-form.

resolved

The open gate below — does $\sigma$-invariance survive with three types? — is now closed. In the coordinate $t=G(b)$ the opponent-reputation law is universally $\Psi(t)=t^2$, strategy-free, exactly as $\Psi=G^2$ in binary. The fixed point dissolves and the co-mixing curve solves in closed form (an $\operatorname{arctanh}$, §6), internally verified by $t\cdot D_{HL}\equiv\text{const}$ (with $D_{HL}$ the High-vs-Low continuation gap, defined in §6c).

1. The object: a signaling all-pay auction

Fix a player of type v. Within a group, two players bid in an all-pay auction; the higher bid advances to the final. Write

  • H_v(b) — the CDF of type v's Round-1 bid, with density h_v(b);
  • the chance of winning Round 1 is the rival's bid CDF, $G(b)=\sum_u f_u\,H_u(b)$, where $f_u$ is the prior probability of type $u$ ($f_u=\tfrac13$ here); the profile of densities $\sigma=(h_L,h_M,h_H)$ is the Round-1 strategy we solve for;
  • the bid is a signal: winning with bid $b$ induces a posterior over your own type, $$p_v(b)=\frac{f_v\,h_v(b)}{\sum_u f_u\,h_u(b)}=\frac{h_v(b)}{\sum_u h_u(b)}\quad(\text{uniform prior cancels}),\qquad\text{\small(post)}$$ a point in the type simplex $\Delta^2$ (two free coordinates, since $p_L{+}p_M{+}p_H{=}1$);
  • the continuation value of reaching the final as type $v$ with reputation $p$ (= the posterior belief vector $p=(p_L,p_M,p_H)$ your winning bid induced) is $$W(v,p)=\int \Pi_2\!\big(v;\,p,\,q\big)\,d\Psi(q),\qquad\text{\small(W)}$$ where $q$ is the opponent finalist's reputation, $\Psi$ its distribution, and $\Pi_2$ the Round-2 payoff — a piecewise-affine function over a 10-cell complex (derived in docs/tropical_cell_structure.html / memory three-type-r2-tropical).

The interim Round-1 payoff of bidding $b$ as type $v$ is

$$U_v(b)\;=\;\underbrace{G(b)}_{\text{win prob}}\cdot\underbrace{W\big(v,\,p(b)\big)}_{\text{prize}}\;-\;\underbrace{b}_{\text{all-pay cost}}.\qquad\text{\small(U)}$$
the twist

The prize $W(v,p(b))$ depends on your own bid through the belief $p(b)$. This is not a Siegel contest, where the prize is a fixed number. It is a signaling all-pay auction — the genus of Hörner & Sahuguet, Costly Signalling in Auctions (2007). Siegel's clean support theory does not apply off the shelf; the question is whether IPV restores enough structure to recover his conclusions anyway.

2. The indifference conditions

Where a type mixes, every bid in its support must give the same payoff: $U_v(b)\equiv\text{const}$. Differentiating in $b$ splits the marginal incentive into two channels:

$$\underbrace{G'(b)\,W\big(v,p(b)\big)}_{\text{win-probability channel}}\;+\;\underbrace{G(b)\,\nabla_p W\big(v,p(b)\big)\cdot p'(b)}_{\text{signaling channel}}\;=\;1.\qquad\text{\small($\star_v$)}$$

Subtracting one type's condition from another cancels the $1$ and leaves the co-mixing (single-crossing) relations $(\star_{vv'})$ — the difference of $(\star_v)$ and $(\star_{v'})$ — one for each active pair, $(\star_{HL})$ and $(\star_{ML})$:

$$G'(b)\,\big[W(v,p)-W(v',p)\big]\;+\;G(b)\,\big[\nabla_p W(v,p)-\nabla_p W(v',p)\big]\cdot p'(b)\;=\;0.\qquad\text{\small($\star_{vv'}$)}$$

These generalize the binary single-crossing $\Delta\tilde W'<0$ from the solved binary case (roadmap §7) to three types ($\tilde W$ throughout denotes the binary-case continuation value — the two-type analogue of $W$, carried over only for comparison); the entire §6 solve is the two instances $(\star_{HL})$, $(\star_{ML})$ integrated. Everything below is about understanding the two ingredients in $(\star_v)$: the signaling channel (governed by the monotone posterior) and the fact that $W$ is the same unknown as the strategy (the obstruction).

3. The monotone posterior — what IPV buys

main focus

The deep-learning Round-1 run confirms the posterior is monotone: as the bid rises, $p_H(b)$ climbs $0.05\to0.65$ and $p_L(b)$ falls $0.59\to0.10$ (the middle share $p_M(b)$ pinned by $p_L{+}p_M{+}p_H{=}1$). High bids signal high types. This single fact drives three reductions.

3a. The signaling channel has a fixed sign

Because $p(b)$ traces a monotone curve through the type simplex, $p'(b)$ never reverses direction — the belief path does not fold back on itself. This alone does not fix the sign of the signaling channel $\nabla_p W(v,p)\cdot p'(b)$. What monotonicity fixes is the direction of travel $\hat d=p'/|p'|$ (a single fixed unit direction, with $|p'|>0$), so the channel factors as $|p'|\cdot\partial_{\hat d}W(v,\cdot)$ — magnitude $|p'|$ times a directional derivative. The sign is then supplied separately by that directional derivative $\partial_{\hat d}W(v,\cdot)$ — negative for High, positive for Low — which is the economics of §3c (and is well-defined only because $W$ is $C^1$, §3b). So: monotonicity is the precondition that makes the projection $\partial_{\hat d}W$ well-defined and steady across the support; §3c makes it signed. Together they yield a fixed-sign signaling channel — and that is what forces each support to be a connected interval rather than fragmented.

3b. The prize is smooth, even though $\Pi_2$ is kinked

The Round-2 payoff $\Pi_2$ is full of kinks (the $\min(\mu,\nu)$, the $(\mu-\nu)^+$). One might fear those kinks make the indifference ODE ill-posed. They do not — because the prize is an average:

key $$W(v,p)=\int \Pi_2(v;p,q)\,d\Psi(q)\quad\Longrightarrow\quad W(v,\cdot)\ \text{is}\ C^1,\quad \nabla_p W=\Psi\big|_{\text{kink locus}}.$$

Integrating a kinked function against an atomless $\Psi$ smooths the kinks away: the kink locus (the set of opponent reputations $q$ where $\Pi_2$ bends — e.g. the diagonal $\mu=\nu$) has $\Psi$-measure zero, so it contributes nothing to the average; the kink cancels and only its slope survives, which is the content of $\nabla_p W=\Psi|_{\text{kink locus}}$ (an envelope / Leibniz computation). This matches the binary identity $\tilde W_H'(\mu)=-2\big(1-\Psi(\mu)\big)$ with $\Psi=G^2$. The kinks of $\Pi_2$ never reach the bidder. So $(\star_v)$ is a genuine, well-posed first-order ODE.

3c. The sign is type-dependent — the engine of "hide and bluff"

Here the signaling channel earns its name. Being perceived as High hurts the High type ($\tilde W_H'<0$): a strong player would rather be underestimated, so the finalist across the table under-prepares. Therefore:

TypeWin-prob channel as $b\uparrow$Signaling channel as $b\uparrow$Behavior
Highprize-weight rises ($G'\!>0$)prize falls (being seen High hurts)channels conflict → hides (bids low)
Lowprize-weight risesprize rises (being seen High helps)channels align → bluffs (bids high)

This is the same mechanism that makes binary bidding non-monotone in realizations, now spread across three types.

why supports are single intervals (the Siegel point)

Monotone $p(b)$ + atomless $\Psi$ make the $(\star_v)$ coefficients monotone and single-crossing in $v$ — precisely the hypothesis an interval-support argument needs. So each type's support is one connected interval $[0,\beta_v]$ in bid space, with a common floor $b_{lo}=0$ and nested ceilings $\beta_H>\beta_M>\beta_L$ (DL, approximate: $0.69>0.62>0.54$; atoms at $0$ grow as type falls: Low $0.49$, Mid $0.30$, High $\approx0$). Symbol caution: §6 works in the quantile coordinate $t=G(b)$ and writes $\beta_L=0.9746$ for the same Low-exit event — the $t$-coordinate of this bid ceiling, not a different number. Bid-space $\beta_L\approx0.54$ and $t$-space $\beta_L=0.9746$ are one exit point in two coordinates.

Contrast with Turocy. Turocy's rich multi-interval / gapped supports need a non-monotone or non-single-crossing prize (correlation / common value). IPV with atomless $\Psi$ rules that out. IPV is exactly what collapses the support to a single interval.

4. The mirror symmetry

needed for the solve

The two groups A and B are interchangeable. Let $\tau$ be the involution that swaps the two finalist roles (own $\leftrightarrow$ opponent, A $\leftrightarrow$ B). On Round-2 regime words it flips every $\mathtt{A}\leftrightarrow\mathtt{B}$.

Under $\tau$, the two Round-2 cells the equilibrium actually visits swap into each other:

cell 1  [B,A,B,A,A]   $\xrightarrow{\ \tau\ }$   cell 2  [A,B,A,B,B]

The diagonal $\mu=\nu$ (own reputation equals opponent reputation) is the fixed locus of $\tau$. In a symmetric equilibrium, both finalists draw their reputation from the same distribution $\Psi$, so the whole configuration is invariant under $\tau$. Three consequences:

  • the equilibrium payoff is invariant under own $\leftrightarrow$ opponent;
  • cells 1 and 2 carry the same payoff form, related by the swap:
    cell$U_A(v_H)$  (own aggregate reputation $A2{=}p_1{+}p_2$, $A3{=}p_2$; opponent $B2{=}q_1{+}q_2$, $B3{=}q_2$, where $p_i,q_i$ are reputation components. In the §6b shares $(m,h)/(mp,hp)$: $A2{=}m{+}h$, $A3{=}h$, $B2{=}mp{+}hp$, $B3{=}hp$ — so this High row equals the §6b cell-1/cell-2 $U_H$.)
    cell 1$2 - B2 - \tfrac13 A3 - \tfrac23 B3$
    cell 2$2 - A2 - \tfrac23 A3 - \tfrac13 B3$
  • the DL run visits only these two cells (~41% each) plus the diagonal wall (~14%); the lopsided corner cells 9,10 are never visited — reputations never get extreme.

Why it matters for solving. We can solve on one side of the diagonal and reflect — gluing cell 1 and cell 2 across $\mu=\nu$, exactly as the binary case glued its two cells across $\mu=\nu$. This collapses the Round-1 state from "full 2-D reputation per finalist over 10 cells" to a 2-piece, mirror-symmetric payoff in one own-vs-opponent variable. It is the single biggest simplification available — and it is legitimate only because the equilibrium is symmetric and stays off the corners.

5. The obstruction

main focus

The trouble is not the kinks (smoothed away, §3b) and not monotonicity (clean, §3a). It is that the prize and the belief are the same unknown.

Look again at what the prize is built from:

$$p_v(b)=\frac{h_v(b)}{\sum_u h_u(b)}\quad\text{and}\quad \Psi=\big(\text{pushforward of }G\text{ through }p(\cdot)\big).$$

Both the posterior $p$ and the opponent-reputation law $\Psi$ are made out of the very densities $h_v$ that the indifference ODE $(\star_v)$ is trying to solve for — and the co-mixing relations $(\star_{vv'})$ inherit the same circularity. So $(\star_v)$, and with it $(\star_{vv'})$, is a fixed-point ODE, not a quadrature: you cannot integrate $W$ as if it were an exogenous prize, because $W$ is defined through the unknown strategy. This circularity is the structural signature of a signaling equilibrium: the interim payoff $G(b)\,W(v,p(b))-b$ looks like an ordinary all-pay objective, but the prize $W$ silently depends on the very strategy being chosen.

How the binary case escaped

In the binary case the continuation turned out to be $\sigma$-invariant: $\Psi=G^2$ pinned $W$ independently of the strategy. The fixed point dissolved, leaving the §7 separable ODE with a first integral and a single scalar

$$G_0=0.2221\quad\text{pinned by}\quad \int_{G_0}^{1}\mu\,dG=\tfrac12,\qquad U_H=2G_0,\quad U_L=0.$$

The entire solvability of the binary model rested on that one fact.

the gate — now resolved (§6)

Does $\sigma$-invariance survive with three types? The 2-cell reduction (§4) makes this checkable: with only cells 1,2 active, $W(v,p)$ is a 2-piece affine functional of $p$, and $\nabla_p W$ enters $(\star_v)$ only through $\Psi$ evaluated at the two cell boundaries. So the whole question reduces to: does the cell-1/cell-2 affine $W$, pushed through the belief map, depend on $\sigma$ only via a scalar (as $G^2$ did)?

Answer: yes — $\sigma$-invariant. In $t=G(b)$, $\Psi(t)=t^2$ universally, so the supports close in the binary form: a few scalars pinned by moment conditions like $\int p\,d\Psi=\text{prior}$, with $[0,\beta_v]$ in closed form. The full curve is in §6.

6. Resolution — $\sigma$-invariance holds, and the curve is closed-form

The concrete next step — set up $(\star_v)$ in the state $t=G(b)$, substitute the cell-1/cell-2 forms and $\Psi$, and test for a first integral — was carried out in Wolfram. The verdict is affirmative.

6a. State, reputation curve, and the universal $\Psi$

Work in $t=G(b)\in[0,1]$ (own win-prob / bid quantile); the population position is $t\sim\text{Uniform}[0,1]$, with the atom spread on $[0,t_0]$, $t_0=G(0)$. Let $m(t),h(t)$ be the Mid/High reputation shares at position $t$ (so $\text{Low}=1-m-h$). The opponent-finalist law is

the $\sigma$-invariance $$\Psi(t)=t^2\quad\text{universally — strategy-free, exactly as }\Psi=G^2\text{ in binary.}\qquad\text{\small($\Psi$)}$$

Why $t^2$: a finalist is a Round-1 winner, so the opponent finalist's position is the population position $s\sim\text{Uniform}[0,1]$ size-biased by its win probability $G=s$ (one rival, 2-player group) — density $2s$, hence CDF $\Psi(t)=\int_0^t 2s\,ds=t^2$. This uses only the bracket structure, not $\sigma$ or the type count, so $W$ is pinned by the curve alone and the Round-1↔Round-2 fixed point dissolves.

6c. Continuation differences and the first integral

Each difference $D_{vL}$ below is $W(v,p)-W(L,p)$ with the continuation $W=\int\Pi_2\,d\Psi$ (eq. (W)) split at the own position $t$: opponent behind ($sahead ($s>t$, cell 2), integrated against $d\Psi=2s\,ds$. The opponent-behind moments are $A_2(t)=\int_0^t 2s\,m\,ds$, $A_3(t)=\int_0^t 2s\,h\,ds$ (both including the atom mass at $t_0$) and $H_3=A_3(1)=\mathbb{E}_{\text{finalist}}[h]$ (integral terms verified to $\sim10^{-15}$):

$$\begin{aligned} W(H)-W(L)&=:D_{HL}=2-m-\tfrac{5h}{3}+\big(\tfrac{m}{2}+\tfrac{2h}{3}\big)t^2-\tfrac{A_2}{2}-\tfrac{2A_3}{3}-\tfrac{H_3}{3},\\[2pt] W(M)-W(L)&=:D_{ML}=1-m-\tfrac{2h}{3}+\big(\tfrac{m}{2}+\tfrac{h}{3}\big)t^2-\tfrac{A_2}{2}-\tfrac{A_3}{3}-\tfrac{H_3}{3}. \end{aligned}$$

Co-mixing of both active types means $t\,D_{HL}=k_1$ and $t\,D_{ML}=k_2$ are constant ($k_1=U_H-U_L$, $k_2=U_M-U_L$) — these are the first integrals of $(\star_{HL})$ and $(\star_{ML})$. Differentiating both in $t$ makes the integral terms $A_2,A_3,H_3$ cancel; subtracting the two eliminates $m$ (this is the $m$-free difference $D_{HM}$ of §6f, which also gives the floor relation $k_1-k_2=t_0$), leaving a clean ODE in $h$ alone:

the closed-form curve $$k_1-k_2=t_0\quad(\text{floor relation}),\qquad h'(t)=\frac{3\,t_0}{t^2\,(3-t^2)},$$ $$\boxed{\,h(t)=1-\frac{t_0}{t}+\frac{t_0}{\sqrt3}\Big(\operatorname{arctanh}\tfrac{t}{\sqrt3}-\operatorname{arctanh}\tfrac{t_0}{\sqrt3}\Big),\quad h(t_0)=0.\,}$$

The $\sqrt3=\sqrt{v_H}$ turns the first integral into an $\operatorname{arctanh}$ — the genuinely new feature versus binary's plain rational; the high valuation enters transcendentally. $m(t)$ follows from a second elementary quadrature (the $C^1$ condition).

Along the NDSolve curve, $t\,D_{HL}(t)=0.4705$ and $t\,D_{ML}(t)=0.1416$ are exactly constant at every $t\in[0.4,1.0]$ — confirming a genuine co-mixing equilibrium, not a fit.

6e. Support structure

Three regions above the atom: atom $[0,0.329]$ (Low+Mid pool, $h=0$); 3-type co-mix $[0.329,\beta_L]$; 2-type (High, Mid) co-mix $[\beta_L,1]$. So the DL-observed nested ceilings are real, not solver noise — just compressed near the top. Since $\text{Mid}(1)=0.240>0$, Mid reaches the very top: no pure-High tail. Reputation: High $0\to0.76$, Mid $0.40\to0.24$, Low $0.60\to0$. (DL was the warm-start approximation — same orderings, magnitudes underestimated; the closed form is now the reference.)

6f. The $[\beta_L,1]$ sliver — closed exactly

On $(\beta_L,1]$ Low has exited, so only High and Mid co-mix and the population constraint becomes $m=1-h$ (Low $=0$). The decisive simplification is that the H–M difference

$m$-free difference $$D_{HM}=D_{HL}-D_{ML}=1-h+\tfrac{h}{3}t^2-\tfrac{A_3}{3}$$

has its $m$ and $A_2$ terms cancel — it depends on $h$ alone. So the H–M co-mixing relation $(\star_{HM})=(\star_{HL})-(\star_{ML})$, in integrated form $t\,D_{HM}=k_1-k_2=t_0$, is an ODE in $h$ only, identical in both regions. Hence $h(t)$ is one global $\operatorname{arctanh}$ curve, $C^1$ across $\beta_L$; $t_0$ and $H_3$ (which see only $h$) are exact and unchanged. Only $m(t)$ switches — to $1-h$ — on the sliver.
