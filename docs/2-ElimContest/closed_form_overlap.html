The overlap solves in closed form — and the equilibrium closes in two regions

Elimination contest, binary $v=3/1$, equal prior. Session 2026-06-22 (revised). Supersedes the "integrate the 2-D ODE with NDSolve" framing of the roadmap §7. Companion: analytic_closed_form_handoff.md.

Revision note. An earlier draft of this page claimed a gluing obstruction — a High type deviating just above a pure-Low atom across an off-path gap $(0,b_{lo})$. That assumed $b_{lo}>0$. There is no gap: $b_{lo}=0$, so there is nothing to deviate into, and the "obstruction" was an artifact of the gap premise. The real correction is to the top (no high-only tail), and the construction is now solved.

1. A first integral collapses the overlap ODE

The roadmap §7 overlap is a self-contained 2-D system in the Round-1 CDF $G$ and the continuation integral $Y(\mu)=\int_0^\mu G^2$:

$$\frac{dG}{d\mu}=\frac{G(2G^2-3)}{3(\mu-1)-2Y},\qquad \frac{dY}{d\mu}=G^2.$$

Define $\Pi(\mu)=G\,(3(\mu-1)-2Y)$ — the denominator times $G$. Along any trajectory

$$\Pi'=G'\,(3(\mu-1)-2Y)+G\,(3-2Y')=G(2G^2-3)+G(3-2G^2)=0,$$

so $\Pi$ is conserved. This is the roadmap's own §3 relation $G\,\Delta\tilde W=U_H-U_L$ re-read: since $\tilde W_L-\tilde W_H=\tfrac23\big(3(\mu-1)-2Y\big)$, the constant is $C=-\tfrac32(U_H-U_L)$. It lived in the document as a monotonicity argument and was never used to integrate the ODE.

$$\Pi(\mu)=G\big(3(\mu-1)-2Y\big)=C\quad\Longrightarrow\quad \frac{dG}{d\mu}=\frac{G^2(2G^2-3)}{C}\ \text{(separable).}$$

The $Y$-equation is gone. Partial fractions integrate it in elementary form:

$$\mu(G)=\mu_0+C\big[\Phi(G)-\Phi(G_0)\big],\qquad \Phi(G)=\frac{1}{3G}-\frac{2}{3\sqrt6}\,\operatorname{arctanh}\!\Big(G\sqrt{\tfrac23}\Big),$$

and everything else is explicit in $G$, with no integral left to carry:

$$Y=\frac{3(\mu-1)}{2}-\frac{C}{2G},\qquad \tilde W_H=(\mu-1)-\frac{C}{G},\qquad \tilde W_L=\tfrac23 Y,$$ $$g=\frac{2G^2-3}{(\mu-1)(2G^2-3)+C/G},\qquad \frac{db}{dG}=(\mu(G)-1)+\frac{C}{G(2G^2-3)}.$$

Verified against direct 2-D integration (scipy, rtol $10^{-11}$): $\Pi$ conserved to $7\times10^{-16}$; the $Y$-free reduced ODE matches the full system to $4\times10^{-16}$; closed-form $\mu(G)$ matches to $8\times10^{-16}$. There is no shooting problem — only a monotone inversion $\mu(G)\to G(\mu)$ and one quadrature for $b$.

2. The bottom boundary: $b_{lo}=0$, hence $\mu_0=0$

All-pay no-gap: if the overlap began at $b_{lo}>0$, leaving $(0,b_{lo})$ empty above the atom, a bidder at $b_{lo}$ could lower its bid into that empty interval at the same win probability $G_0$ and the same continuation, but lower cost — a strict gain. So no $b_{lo}>0$ survives, and the overlap is flush against the atom, $b_{lo}=0$.

Low's break-even at the overlap bottom is $G_0\,\tilde W_L(\mu_0)-b_{lo}=U_L=0$. With $b_{lo}=0$ and $G_0>0$ this forces $\tilde W_L(\mu_0)=0$, and since $\tilde W_L(\mu)=\tfrac23\int_0^\mu G^2$ vanishes only at $0$,

$$b_{lo}=0\quad\Longrightarrow\quad \mu_0=0\quad\Longrightarrow\quad C=-3G_0,\quad U_H=2G_0.$$

The belief starts at $0$ continuously — no off-path gap and no belief jump. The pure-Low atom fixes $U_L=\tfrac12 G_0\tilde W_L(0)=0$.

3. The top: no high-only tail

A high-only tail would carry belief $\equiv1$, so the overlap would have to reach $\mu=1$. It cannot: from the closed form, $\mu=1$ needs $\Phi(G)=\Phi(G_0)-\tfrac{1}{3G_0}<0$, which occurs only at $G>1$ (where $G\sqrt{2/3}\to1$ and $\operatorname{arctanh}\to\infty$), outside the CDF range. So no bid is ever revealed-High.

And none needs to be. $\tilde W_H'(\mu)=-2(1-G^2)<0$: being perceived High hurts in Round 2, so High has no reason to bid up and separate. Both types co-mix to $b_{top}$, where $G=1$ at belief $\mu_{\max}<1$. A bid above $b_{top}$ already wins with probability $1$ and only adds cost, and any off-path belief gives $\tilde W_H\le\tilde W_H(\mu_{\max})$ — the deviation dies without a tail.

The support is two regions: a pure-Low atom at $0$, then a co-mixing overlap $[0,b_{top}]$ with belief rising $0\to\mu_{\max}$. No gap, no jump, no tail.

4. Closure — one unknown, one equation (solved)

High carries no atom, so its entire unit of mass sits on the overlap:

$$\int_0^{b_{top}}\! f_H\,db=2\!\int_{G_0}^{1}\!\mu(G)\,dG=1 \quad\Longrightarrow\quad \int_{G_0}^{1}\!\mu(G)\,dG=\tfrac12,$$

which pins $G_0$. Low's normalization is then automatic: $2\int_{G_0}^1(1-\mu)\,dG=1-2G_0$ and $a_L=2G_0$ give $1$. Two normalizations collapse to one.

$$G_0=0.2221,\quad a_L=0.4443,\quad \mu_{\max}=0.9525,\quad b_{top}=0.1746,\quad U_H=2G_0=0.4443,\quad U_L=0.$$

Both indifference levels check at both ends, by independent evaluations agreeing to four decimals. For $U_H$: at the atom edge $(\mu=0,b=0)$, $G_0\tilde W_H(0)=2G_0=0.4443$; integrating to the top, $\tilde W_H(\mu_{\max})-b_{top}=0.4443$. For $U_L$: $0$ at the atom, and $\tilde W_L(\mu_{\max})-b_{top}=0.0000$ at the top. Indifference holds end-to-end; the construction is closed.

Against the M200 grid sim: the grid solver is expected to differ from the exact solution and is also under-converged / over-spread (~40%). It widens the overlap to $[0.015,0.392]$, shrinking its atom to $0.122$ and reading $U_H\approx0.369$; its apparent "high-only tail" carries belief only $\approx0.87$ — the smear of one co-mixing region, not a real tail.
