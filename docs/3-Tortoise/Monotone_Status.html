Monotonicity status: what is proven

2026-06-22, updated 2026-07-09. A single-page map of the chain-monotonicity results for Tortoise σ(m,n) all-pay contests, across cost classes: what is closed, what is open, where the primary writeups live, and what to do next. Prerequisite: QUICKSTART §0 (notation) and §2 (the σ(m,n) FOC + indifference system). For a from-scratch tutorial on the comb march itself, read Docs/comb_march_tutorial.html first.

Summary — the state of play in three tiers. Tier 1, quadratic cost $c(e)=e^2$: chain monotonicity is fully PROVEN, in every asymmetry regime and at every rung, by one closed recursion whose derivatives telescope into a ladder of positive factors. Tier 2, cubic cost: also PROVEN — first for $c(e)=e^3$ (2026-07-07, the "margin field" theorem) and, after the 2026-07-09 CAS closure, for every strictly convex cubic. This was the first complete route beyond quadratic. Tier 3, everything else: open but sharply reduced. The 2026-07-08/09 quartic campaign both closed the friendly half numerically (all coefficients $\ge0$: one polynomial inequality (SUFF6′) away from a proof) and found — then resolved — a genuine hole in the unfriendly half ($b<0$): the per-node inequality this program had been chasing is actually false on dials the theorem visits, yet everything the theorem consumes survives with growing margin. Consequence: for general costs the proof burden moves from "every atom's mass grows with the dial" to the aggregate "$F'>0$" — which is precisely the one lemma the new homotopy route was already built around.

1. What "monotonicity" means here, in plain words

Each equilibrium σ(m,n) is a "rung" on a ladder: deeper rungs mean both players spread their bids over more atoms, competition is fiercer, and the leader (the tortoise) should keep less rent. Chain monotonicity is that statement made precise: the leader's equilibrium payoff $u_1^\ast$ falls as you descend the ladder of rungs. It is what connects the finite theory to the competitive limit.

How one proves it: the comb march builds the whole equilibrium candidate forward from a single input, the dial $t:=q_1$ (the mass the follower puts on her bottom atom). Turning the dial up makes every atom's mass grow; the equilibrium dial $q_1^\ast$ for rung $(m,n)$ is where the total mass hits exactly 1 — the root of the normalization defect $F(t)=\sum_\ell q_\ell(t)-1$. The operational targets (chain_monotonicity_reduction.md) are two "everything moves the right way when you turn the dial" facts about the off-equilibrium comb:

  • Gap-widening: every gap between neighboring atoms opens as the dial turns: $\delta_k'(t)>0$, $d_k'(t)>0$.
  • Mass-widening: every atom's mass grows with the dial: $p_k'(t)\ge0$, $q_k'(t)\ge0$. Summing gives $F'(t)>0$, so $F$ crosses zero once — the equilibrium dial is unique, and the roots of successive rungs are ordered, which is what makes the payoff ladder monotone.

Gap-widening and $F'>0$ both follow from mass-widening (this step is cost-general), so for a long time the whole program rested on one per-node sign: the node residual $\mathrm{RG2}_k\ge0$, which is the statement "atom $k+1$'s mass grows". The 2026-07-09 finding (§3.5 below) shows that per-node sign is false for some admissible costs, while the aggregate $F'>0$ it was meant to deliver is not — so the honest target is now the aggregate, and this page tracks both.

All four regimes are one theorem, and the reason quadratic is easy is worth stating plainly: when $c(e)=e^2$, marginal cost is a straight line, so every atom is a rational function of the dial and the whole comb obeys one closed recursion. Normalizing $v_2=1$ (so $w_1=s=\sqrt{v_1}$, $f=1-s-\Delta$), the value asymmetry $s$ is the only asymmetry symbol inside the recursion, and the head start $\Delta$ enters only through the very first gap:

$$\delta_k=\frac{A_{k-1}\,d_{k-1}(d_{k-1}+s)}{s\,A_k},\qquad d_k=\frac{B_{k-1}\,\delta_k(\delta_k+1)}{B_k},\qquad \delta_1=\frac{t(1-\Delta)}{1+t}+(s-1)+\Delta.$$

Differentiating in $\log t$ makes the unsigned cumulative terms cancel (telescoping), leaving a ladder in which each derivative is the previous one times a manifestly positive factor:

$$\delta_k'=\delta_k\cdot\frac{s\,d_{k-1}'}{d_{k-1}(d_{k-1}+s)},\qquad d_k'=d_k\cdot\frac{\delta_k'}{\delta_k(\delta_k+1)},$$

seeded by $\delta_1'=(1-\Delta)/(1+t)^2>0$ (needs only $\Delta<1$, and does not involve $s$ at all). Positivity then propagates down the ladder for free; mass-widening follows by the product rule; $F'>0$ follows by summing. In the symmetric case everything collapses to the one-liner $G_i=t/(1+it)$, $G_i'=(1+it)^{-2}>0$.

Three facts worth carrying into the paper: (1) the asymmetries factor cleanly — $s$ deforms the ladder's multipliers, $(s,\Delta)$ shift the base, and the seed $\delta_1'$ sees only $\Delta$; (2) the lead axis is the easy direction ($\Delta$ is a single scalar dent in the first gap; the recursion itself is pure-symmetric) — the old intuition "value hard, lead easy" was backwards; (3) monotonicity decouples from existence: the $\Delta<1$ bound is far looser than the existence-death threshold (e.g. $\Delta^\ast(2,2)\approx0.211$). All identities are exact-symbolic verified over $\mathbb{Q}(s,\Delta,t)$ through $(5,5)$ [ladder] / $(4,4)$ [mass].

Quadratic caveat. The §3 telescoping/ladder/mass identities are machine-verified but not yet hand-checked; that hand-verification, plus folding the four regimes into one stated paper theorem, is the remaining bookkeeping (a lower-priority Next item).

3. General strictly convex cost — cubic PROVEN, rest OPEN but reduced

Primary records: rg2_positivity_attack.md (the frontier doc — start at its §0 checklist), theta3_monotonicity_theorem.md (the standalone cubic theorem), general_cost_monotonicity_handoff.md §4.9 (the bridge from the old plan). The power family $c(e)=e^\theta$ is the test bed; §3.5 covers quartics and beyond.

Why the quadratic proof does not transfer. The atoms are rational functions of the dial iff marginal cost $c'$ is affine iff $c$ is quadratic. For any other cost the atoms are genuinely algebraic — there is no closed recursion over $\mathbb{Q}(t)$ and nothing for the log-derivative ladder to telescope. A different idea was needed; it came in three steps (§3.1–§3.3).

Groundwork that survives from the first attack (triangular-Jacobian analysis):

  • Local solvability is free. Each march node is a $2\times2$ root-find whose Jacobian is lower-triangular with positive diagonal, forced by convexity alone; the analytic derivative assembly reproduces finite differences to $\sim2\times10^{-9}$ ($\theta=2,3,4$).
  • Everything reduces to mass-widening. Atom positions move the right way automatically once masses do, so gap-widening and $F'>0$ all hang on the single per-node sign $\mathrm{RG2}_k\ge0$.
  • The offset that pays for it. $\mathrm{RG2}$ is positive only thanks to the envelope-theorem term $u_1'=v_1-c(m_0)>0$, constant across nodes; the march identities alone are circular, so any proof must import an inequality from outside the identity algebra. That impossibility result (2026-07-07) is what forced the margin-field reformulation below.

3.1 The margin field: turning a node inequality into a picture

The reformulation replaces "is this residual number nonnegative?" with "is this function nonnegative on an interval?" — which has shape, and shape can be argued. Fix node $k$ and look at the interval $[x_k,x_{k+1}]$ between two consecutive leader atoms. Two natural fields live there (margins $r_\ell(z)=w_1+Y_\ell-z$):

$$G_k(z)=c'(z)-\sum_{\ell\le k}q_\ell\,c'(r_\ell(z)),\qquad \Phi_k(z)=-\partial_t G_k(z)=\sum_{\ell\le k}\Big[q_\ell'\,c'(r_\ell(z))+q_\ell\,c''(r_\ell(z))\,Y_\ell'\Big].$$

$G_k$ is the FOC field (it vanishes at $x_k$ — that is the first-order condition — and rises with slope equal to the stiffness $W_k(z)=c''(z)+\sum q_\ell c''(r_\ell(z))>0$); $\Phi_k$ is the flux (how fast the FOC level drifts as the dial turns). The key integral identity (machine-verified to $2\times10^{-15}$) writes the node residual as the integral of a pointwise margin:

$$\mathrm{RG2}_k=\int_{x_k}^{x_{k+1}} D_k(z)\,dz,\qquad D_k(z)=Y_{k+1}'\,W_k(z)-\Phi_k(z).$$

In words: mass-widening at node $k$ asks whether the frontier atom's motion, weighted by stiffness, outruns the flux on average over the interval. Three structural facts make the frame work:

  • The induction hypothesis sits at the left endpoint: $D_k(x_k)=W_k(x_k)\,\gamma_{k+1}'$ — gap-widening from the previous node is literally the margin's starting value, so the induction hands each node a nonnegative start.
  • Gap-widening becomes a shape condition $(M_k)$: $\bar D_k\ \ge\ \theta_k\,D_k(x_{k+1})$, where $\bar D_k$ is the mean margin and $\theta_k=M_1/(h_kM_0)$ is the interval's stiffness-moment centroid.
  • Two exact identities control the shape: the trapezoid-error identity (E1) $$\theta_k-\tfrac12=-\frac{1}{2h_kM_0}\int_{x_k}^{x_{k+1}}(z-x_k)(x_{k+1}-z)\,W_k'(z)\,dz,$$ so "centroid below ½" is exactly "stiffness-slope $W_k'\ge0$ on average"; and the endpoint split (E2) $D(r)-D(l)=Y_{k+1}'\,\Delta W+J_0$ with $J_0\ge0$ free under the IH.

3.2 The on-window realization

The single most consequential observation of 2026-07-07 is also the simplest. The theorem only ever needs dials up to the equilibrium root — the window $\mathcal{W}=\{F\le0\}$ — never beyond. On $\mathcal{W}$ every partial mass satisfies $Q_k=\sum_{\ell\le k}q_\ell<1$ and $P_k<1$, simply because $Q_k=(1+F)-\sum_{\ell>k}q_\ell<1+F\le1$ — a consequence of the value of $F$, not of its monotonicity, so nothing is circular. Every numerical "counterexample" the program had logged up to then turned out to live off-window. The right question was never "does the inequality hold everywhere?" but "does it hold where the theorem needs it?" (This lesson repeats, with the opposite outcome, in §3.5: the quartic corner is the first violation that survives the on-window test.)

3.3 The cubic theorem — PROVEN (theta3_monotonicity_theorem.md)

At $c(e)=e^3$ the two cost facts $c'''\equiv6$, $c''''\equiv0$ collapse every shape question into the window fact $Q_k<1$:

$$W_k'(z)\equiv 6(1-Q_k)>0,\qquad \theta_k-\tfrac12=-\frac{(1-Q_k)h_k^2}{2M_0}<0,\qquad D_k''=-6\sum_{\ell\le k}q_\ell'\le0.$$

So on-window the margin $D_k$ is concave with nonnegative endpoints (left = IH; right = left $+\,Y'\Delta W+J_0$, all $\ge0$), hence $D_k\ge0$ pointwise, hence $\mathrm{RG2}_k\ge0$; and concavity gives $\bar D\ge\tfrac12(D(l)+D(r))\ge\theta_k D(r)$, which is $(M_k)$, i.e. gap-widening. The mirror argument is verbatim ($V'=6(1-P_k)$), the base case is the $k=0$ instance of the same integral identity with empty flux, and the induction closes: mass-widening at every node, $F'>0$, unique dial, chain monotonicity. Every step is an exact identity (verified $10^{-13}$–$10^{-16}$) plus a sign — no estimates anywhere.

Update 2026-07-09 (CAS closure): the symbolic skeleton is now machine algebra end to end — field identities, telescoping, assembly, mirror pack, base case — for the general convex cubic $ae^3+be^2+c_0e$ (Julia/converge/_cubic_symbolic_cas2.wl §G1–§G6b, clean run, all residuals 0). The base-case gap-widening reduces to cost log-concavity, which every convex cubic satisfies (the transient "$c_0$-hole" was retracted), so the earlier $b,c\ge0$ restriction is lifted: chain monotonicity holds for every strictly convex cubic. Only the chain-assembly step is still inherited from the quadratic write-up rather than CAS-verified. Worklist: cubic_symbolic_gaps_worklist.md.

3.4 Power costs $\theta\ne2,3$ — what remains, regime by regime

Powers $\theta\in(2,3)$ — the hard regime. Here $c'''$ is decreasing, so old depleted margins fight the transfer from the FOC (a $c'$-level constraint) to the stiffness slope (a $c'''$-level claim). This is genuinely delicate: pointwise $W'\ge0$ fails on-window in deep combs ($\theta=2.25$, $m\ge12$), and even the clean centroid bound $\theta_k\le\tfrac12$ crosses at last nodes for $m\ge20$ (excess $\sim10^{-6}$). The surviving target is the buffered centroid lemma

$$\text{(M-buf)}\qquad \theta_k-\tfrac12\ \le\ \frac{D_k(x_k)}{2\,D_k(x_{k+1})},$$

and here the frame pays off: (E1) gives $\theta_k-\tfrac12\le h\cdot\max(-W')_+/(12\bar W)$ — the excess carries a free factor of the atom spacing $h$, which shrinks as combs deepen, while the buffer stays flat at $O(0.15)$. At the observed crossing there are five orders of magnitude of room. Plus one endpoint lemma (TR-a): $D(r)\ge0$ (empirically $\ge2.4$, never close).

Powers $\theta>3$ — the easy regime (one lemma missing). For $\theta\ge3$ the transfer inequality reverses direction: Hölder against the FOC constraint gives, on-window,

$$\sum_{\ell\le k}q_\ell\,r_\ell^{\theta-3}\ \le\ Q_k^{2/(\theta-1)}\,z^{\theta-3}\ <\ z^{\theta-3} \qquad\Longrightarrow\qquad W_k'\ge0\ \text{ FREE},$$

so the centroid bound and $D(r)\ge D(l)$ come for free at every $\theta\ge3$ — the same transfer that is provably false for $\theta<3$. What breaks is only the concavity step: $c''''>0$ makes $D$ mildly convex. Numerically everything the theorem needs still holds with slack at $\theta=4,5,6,7$ on-window ($m\le16$), and the slack grows with depth. Missing: a bounded-convexity replacement for the concavity argument, with the Hölder slack $1-Q^{2/(\theta-1)}$ as the budget.

Powers $\theta<2$ swap the hard lemmas (regime duality): the shape/centroid side comes free while right-endpoint positivity becomes the fight. First systematic sub-quadratic data (2026-07-09, homotopy probes): $\theta=1.9$ is chain-monotone through $m\le12$. Beyond probes, unexplored.

$\theta=2$ re-proof as sanity: the quadratic case is the degenerate instance of the same machine — $W'\equiv0$, $D$ linear, $\theta_k\equiv\tfrac12$ — and the whole §3.3 argument collapses to one line per node, reproducing the ladder proof.

3.5 Quartics and beyond — the 2026-07-08/09 campaign

Power costs have $c'''$ of one sign. Quartics $c=Ae^4+Be^3+D_2e^2$ are the first family where $c'''=24Ae+6B$ can change sign ($B<0$), and that turned out to be exactly the fault line. The campaign split the family in two.

All coefficients $\ge0$ (sub-problem A) — numerically closed, one inequality from proof. For $B\ge0$ a cascade argument (quartic_monotonicity_findings.md §3bis) closes gap- and mass-widening numerically: a composite margin is bounded below along the march, its audited floor is $1.2305$ (268 cells, 0 deaths; the earlier "$\ge2.48$" and "$1.52$" figures were artifacts, both retracted), and — the useful surprise — the binding case is always the base node, so the cascade floor is lemma L1's infimum: prove L1 and the whole sub-problem follows. L1 was then symbolically reduced (L1_symbolic_reduction.md, 2026-07-09): seven lemmas are now exact (31/31 Wolfram checks, generic coefficients), and everything rests on one radical-free polynomial inequality (SUFF6′), numerical margin $1.1828$, zero violations on a 71275-point grid, unproved. Two supporting cascade lemmas (L3 dichotomy, L5 telescoped closure) also remain.

Sign-crossing $c'''$ ($B<0$) — a real hole, found and resolved. Near the convexity budget ($D_2\lesssim 1.1\times 3B^2/8A$) the curvature $c''$ has a nearly-degenerate interior dip. When a few early atoms straddle that dip, the per-node inequality $\mathrm{RG2}_k\ge0$ genuinely fails — on thin dial bands at $t\lesssim2.6\times10^{-4}$, one band per atom index crossing the dip (the 1.86 audit, rg2_dial_domain_finding.md). The natural hope was that such tiny dials are never visited by the theorem. That hope is false: the march is a pure forward recursion, so rung windows tile the whole dial axis, and the violation was certified on-window — the worst dial sits at 99.98% of the equilibrium dial of rung $m=1220$ (yes, the ladder reaches that deep: $t^\ast(m)\sim C/m^{1.5}$). The pointwise induction hypothesis also flips there (at exactly one atom).

Why this is a redirection, not a defeat. Everything the chain argument actually consumes was certified to survive at the violation, with margin that grows with depth: the aggregate $F'=\sum q'_\ell$ stays above the $q_1'=1$ subsidy at every rung tested ($\le1800$) and is $\approx+4180$ at the exposed rungs against a deficit of $\sim10^{-3}$ at one atom; $F$ single-crosses; the roots stay ordered; the payoff ladder stays monotone. Mechanism: near a deep root $F'\sim O(m)$ while the corner deficit is $O(10^{-3})$ at a handful of nodes — scale separation. Verdict (rg2_dial_domain_finding.md §8, handoff rg2_dial_domain_handoff.md): per-node $\mathrm{RG2}\ge0$ is dead as an all-costs lemma; the proof must target the aggregate $F'>0$ / single-crossing directly. Quadratic, all convex cubics, $\theta$-powers, and $B\ge0$ quartics are structurally immune (no dip to straddle).

The homotopy route — where the aggregate target already lives. Independently (2026-07-09), a cascade-free continuation strategy was opened: deform the cost along $c_s=(1-s)e^2+s\,c$ from the proven quadratic to the target cost and track the equilibrium. Its single remaining lemma is precisely "$F'>0$ at zeros of $F$" — the aggregate quantity that never fails, even at the $B<0$ corner. Rung death along the path was falsified-but-absorbed (rectangle rungs can die inside the convex family; squares survive; interiority absorbs the holes). The dial-domain resolution above independently validates this as the right formulation. Read homotopy_route_handoff.md + homotopy_route_probe.md.

Arbitrary degree — a warning from the base case. The base-case lemma "$b_1\ge0$" is equivalent to cost log-concavity $c'^2-c''c\ge0$, which is free through degree 5 but dies at degree $\ge6$ (exact sweep: $e^n+ae^2$ fails for $n\ge6$). So for high degrees even the base case is march-geometry, not algebra; the worst case is the one-parameter scaling slice with limiting cost $\hat c(z)=z^n+z^2$. Read arbitrary_degree_transfer_insights.md before attacking any degree $\ge5$.
