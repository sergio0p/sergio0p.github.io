Why we're not scooped

The verdict rests on one structural fact, reached along four independent axes.

The one-line kill

All three rivals predict a single equilibrium payoff. We prove a whole ranked family of them. Priority cannot attach to a phenomenon a paper does not exhibit.

Winner rule

We are a deterministic all-pay contest. Ryvkin is noisy — and that noise is precisely what smooths away the kink our multiplicity depends on. Our model is his σ→0 limit.

Cost

We use a general convex per-round cost, so how effort splits across rounds is payoff-relevant. Seel and Clark–Nilssen are linear; Ryvkin is quadratic-flow. None share the substitution motive.

The kink

Our engine is the payoff-relevant lead magnitude that kinks the continuation. Seel removes it (effort unobservable), Ryvkin smooths it (noise), Clark–Nilssen delete it (binary win-count).

Output

We deliver a ranked family built by a recursion and disciplined by comparative statics. Each rival delivers a single closed-form equilibrium — no ranking, no construction, no spectrum.
