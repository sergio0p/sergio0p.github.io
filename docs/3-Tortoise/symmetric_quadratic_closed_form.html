Symmetric-quadratic σ(m,n): universal closed form of the comb

2026-06-21. Companion to Docs/symmetric_quadratic_closed_form.md. Prerequisite: QUICKSTART §0 (notation, truncation rule) and §2 (the σ(m,n) FOC + indifference system) — nothing else.

Summary: In the symmetric quadratic case ($v_1=v_2=1$, $\Delta=0$, $c(e)=e^2$) every object of the off-equilibrium forward march — driven by the single dial $t:=q_1$ — has an explicit closed form. Each comb gap is $G_i = t/(1+i\,t)$, with $i$ the gap's ordinal position from the left edge. Hence gap-widening $G_i' = (1+it)^{-2} > 0$ is a one-liner, masses are manifestly increasing in $t$, so $F'(q_1)>0$ and chain monotonicity ($q_1^\ast$ decreasing $\Rightarrow u_1^\ast$ decreasing) follows. This resolves the open "two-sided barycenter coupling" nub of chain_monotonicity_reduction.md §6 — in closed form, not by a coupled induction. 1. The universal march (off-equilibrium, dial $t=q_1$)

For $v_1=v_2=1$, $\Delta=0$, $c=e^2$, reach $w=1$, shift $f=0$, the QUICKSTART §2 system is triangular with the single source $t=q_1$; everything else is explicit and no normalization is imposed (that is what "off-equilibrium" means). In moment form, with $g_\ell=1+y_\ell$, $h_k=1+x_k$, and cumulatives $Q_k=\sum_{\ell\le k}q_\ell$, $S_k=\sum_{\ell\le k}q_\ell g_\ell$, $P_k=\sum_{i\le k}p_i$, $T_k=\sum_{i\le k}p_i h_i$:

$$x_k = \frac{S_k}{1+Q_k},\qquad y_\ell = \frac{T_{\ell-1}}{1+P_{\ell-1}},$$ $$q_{k+1} = (1+Q_k)\,d_k(d_k+2),\ \ d_k=y_{k+1}-x_k,\qquad p_k = (1+P_{k-1})\,\delta_k(\delta_k+2),\ \ \delta_k=x_k-y_k.$$

March order: $t \to x_1 \to p_1 \to y_2 \to q_2 \to x_2 \to p_2 \to y_3 \to \cdots$, each entry an explicit rational function of $t$. The rung $(m,n)$ only fixes where you stop and which leftover mass closes $\sum=1$; the atom / gap / mass functions of $t$ below are rung-independent.

2. Main result — closed-form comb gaps

Index the gaps consecutively by comb position $i$ along $y_1=0 < x_1 < y_2 < x_2 < \cdots$. Positions $1,2,3,4,5,6,\dots$ are $\delta_1, d_1, \delta_2, d_2, \delta_3, d_3, \dots$; in general position $2j-1$ is $\delta_j$ and position $2j$ is $d_j$. Then

$$\boxed{\,G_i \;=\; \frac{t}{1+i\,t}\,}\qquad\Longrightarrow\qquad G_i' \;=\; \frac{1}{(1+i\,t)^2} \;>\; 0\quad (t>0),$$

equivalently $\delta_j = t/(1+(2j-1)t)$ and $d_j = t/(1+2j\,t)$. The atoms are partial sums of the gaps (comb point $i$ sits at $\sum_{l\le i}G_l$):

$$x_k = \sum_{l=1}^{2k-1}\frac{t}{1+l\,t},\qquad y_k = \sum_{l=1}^{2k-2}\frac{t}{1+l\,t}.$$

Only $x_1 = t/(1+t)$ was previously known; the rest is new.

3. Closed-form masses and cumulatives

Cumulative masses (verified exact):

$$1+Q_n = (1+t)\prod_{l=1}^{n-1}\!\left(\frac{1+(2l+1)t}{1+2l\,t}\right)^{2},\qquad 1+P_n = \prod_{l=1}^{n}\!\left(\frac{1+2l\,t}{1+(2l-1)t}\right)^{2}.$$

These are the §6 telescoping identity $(1+d_j)^2=(1+Q_{j+1})/(1+Q_j)$ (and its $P$-mirror), now solved rather than merely stated. The atom masses follow from the mass-update with the closed-form gaps:

$$q_k = (1+Q_{k-1})\,\frac{t\,(2+(4k-3)t)}{(1+(2k-2)t)^2},\qquad p_k = (1+P_{k-1})\,\frac{t\,(2+(4k-1)t)}{(1+(2k-1)t)^2},\qquad q_1=t.$$

Fully expanded:

$$q_k = t(1+t)(2+(4k-3)t)\frac{\prod_{l=1}^{k-2}(1+(2l+1)t)^2}{\prod_{l=1}^{k-1}(1+2l\,t)^2}\ (k\ge2),\qquad p_k = t(2+(4k-1)t)\frac{\prod_{l=1}^{k-1}(1+2l\,t)^2}{\prod_{l=1}^{k}(1+(2l-1)t)^2}.$$

Every factor is positive and increasing in $t$ on $t>0$.

4. Consequences — the implication ladder, now all discharged

1. Gap-widening (the §6 "single remaining nub"): $G_i'=(1+it)^{-2}>0$. No coupling argument. The two-sided barycenter coupling was an artifact of signing $y_{j+1}'-x_j'$ as a difference of separate atom-derivatives; the difference $d_j'$ itself has numerator $1$.

2. Mass-monotonicity: $q_k, p_k$ are products of positive increasing factors, so $q_k'>0$, $p_k'>0$.

3. $F'(q_1)>0$: each rung's residual is $\sum(\text{recursion-determined masses})-1$, a sum of increasing terms, so $F'>0$ — single-crossing a fortiori. ($F$ is defined in chain_monotonicity_reduction.md §1.)

5. Schur complement / regularity: $F'(q_1)$ is the Schur complement of the equilibrium Jacobian (reduction doc §4), so $F'>0$ globally also yields Theorem-1 regularity and Theorem-3 uniqueness in this regime.

So in the symmetric quadratic case the entire monotonicity program collapses to the single closed form $G_i=t/(1+it)$.

5. Status — what is and isn't established

Established (exact over $\mathbb{Q}(t)$): all closed forms above hold for every comb position checked — $d_1\dots d_5$ and $\delta_1\dots\delta_6$ (positions 1–13), masses and cumulatives to index 6. The pattern is transparent arithmetic.

Not yet a theorem: $G_i=t/(1+it)$ is a conjecture for general $i$. What is needed is an induction — assume the closed forms through position $i$, push one FOC and one indifference step, confirm position $i+1$ yields gap $t/(1+(i+1)t)$. Equivalently, plug the §2/§3 ansatz into the full QUICKSTART §2 system and verify each equation type as an identity in $(t,k)$ with $k$ symbolic. Expected routine given the structure, but unproven.

q[1]=t; y[1]=0; Qc=q[1]; Sc=q[1](1+y[1]); Pc=0; Tc=0;

Do[ x[k]=Together[Sc/(1+Qc)];

  dl=x[k]-y[k]; p[k]=Together[(1+Pc) dl (dl+2)];

  Pc=Together[Pc+p[k]]; Tc=Together[Tc+p[k](1+x[k])];

  y[k+1]=Together[Tc/(1+Pc)];

  dk=y[k+1]-x[k]; q[k+1]=Together[(1+Qc) dk (dk+2)];

  Qc=Together[Qc+q[k+1]]; Sc=Together[Sc+q[k+1](1+y[k+1])];

,{k,1,6}];

7. Methodological note — why this was missed

The §6 attack on gap-widening signed the difference of two coupled barycenter-derivatives $y_{j+1}'$ and $x_j'$ — the "two-sided coupling," declared the open crux. Computing the gap $d_j = y_{j+1}-x_j$ directly as a function of $t$ (rather than differencing the atoms) removes the coupling entirely: $d_j=t/(1+2jt)$. The trigger was a ratio probe ($y_{j+1}'/x_j'$ versus $1$); the ratio is clean ($(\text{ratio}-1)$ numerator $=\prod(1+kt)^2$), but the difference is cleaner still (numerator $1$). Lesson: sign the gap, not the two atoms.
