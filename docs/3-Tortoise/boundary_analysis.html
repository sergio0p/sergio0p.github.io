The seven boundary faces of $\mathcal D$ σ(2,2) reduced system — what each wall of the ordering-and-mass domain forces, on $\overline{\mathcal D}$ with $F(z)=0$.

One engine drives every result: injectivity of $c$ and $c'$. Strict convexity gives $c(s)=c(t)\Rightarrow s=t$ and $c'(s)=c'(t)\Rightarrow s=t$; on the closure the ordering may bind, squeezing a free atom. Each face is pushed through $F=0$ until it either cascades to a corner, collapses the configuration, or pins a single parameter surface. The discovery: these are not seven independent walls — they chain into one another, and every one of them is a piece of $\partial R_{2,2}$.

The system $F(z)=0$,   $z=(x_1,x_2,\tilde y_2,\,p_1,p_2,\,q_1,q_2)$

Atoms $y_1{=}0,\,y_2$ (P2) and $x_1,x_2$ (P1) interleave in the $f$-shifted comb $\tilde y_1\le x_1\le \tilde y_2\le x_2$, where $\tilde y_\ell=y_\ell+f$, $f=w_2-w_1-\Delta$ (so $\tilde y_1=f$; symmetric $f=0$ gives $0\le x_1\le y_2\le x_2$). P1 places mass $p_1$ at $x_1$, $p_2$ at $x_2$; P2 places $q_1$ at $y_1{=}0$, $q_2$ at $y_2=\tilde y_2-f$. P2 is squeezed to $u_2=0$; $u_1$ is eliminated (INDX is the difference $U_1(x_2)-U_1(x_1)$). In $\tilde y$ the clash-gaps are $f$-free — P1 wins a clash at $s=w_1+\tilde y_\ell-x_k$, P2 at $t=w_2+x_k-\tilde y_\ell$ — so $f$ survives only inside P2's own first-round cost $c(\tilde y_2-f)=c(y_2)$. Writing every gap out in full:

$$\begin{aligned} (\text{FOCX}_1)\quad & q_1\,c'(w_1+\tilde y_1-x_1)-c'(x_1)=0\\ (\text{FOCX}_2)\quad & q_1\,c'(w_1+\tilde y_1-x_2)+q_2\,c'(w_1+\tilde y_2-x_2)-c'(x_2)=0\\ (\text{FOCY}_2)\quad & p_1\,c'(w_2+x_1-\tilde y_2)-c'(\tilde y_2-f)=0\\ (\text{INDX})\quad & c(x_1)-c(x_2)-q_1\big(v_1-c(w_1+\tilde y_1-x_1)\big)+q_1\big(v_1-c(w_1+\tilde y_1-x_2)\big)+q_2\big(v_1-c(w_1+\tilde y_2-x_2)\big)=0\\ (\text{INDY}_2)\quad & -c(\tilde y_2-f)+p_1\big(v_2-c(w_2+x_1-\tilde y_2)\big)=0\\ (\text{M}_p)\quad & 1-p_1-p_2=0,\qquad (\text{M}_q)\ \ 1-q_1-q_2=0 \end{aligned}$$

Standing assumptions. $c$ strictly convex, $c(0)=0$, $c'>0$ and strictly increasing on $(0,\infty)$, with $c'(0)=0$ (cost family $c(e)=e^{r}/r,\ r>1$; quadratic $e^2/2$). The clash-gaps $s,t$ are $\ge 0$, and $>0$ where positivity is invoked. If instead $c'(0)>0$, the three mass-collapse faces below are simply empty — an even cleaner non-escape; flagged where relevant.

1 · The lower stratum: $\;x_1=0\ \Longleftrightarrow\ q_1=0$ equivalence

Faces $\{x_1=0\}$ and $\{q_1=0\}$ are the same wall.

$x_1=0\Rightarrow q_1=0$. Put $x_1=0$ in FOCX$_1$:

  1. $q_1\,c'(w_1+\tilde y_1-x_1)=c'(x_1)=c'(0)=0$,   with $w_1+\tilde y_1-x_1\big|_{x_1=0}=w_1+f>0\Rightarrow c'(\cdot)>0$.  Hence $\;q_1=0$.

$q_1=0\Rightarrow x_1=0$. Put $q_1=0$ in FOCX$_1$: $\;-c'(x_1)=0\Rightarrow c'(x_1)=0\Rightarrow x_1=0$. The two faces coincide.

On this stratum ($q_1=0,\ q_2=1$, P2 a single atom at $y_2$):

  1. FOCX$_2$: $\;c'(w_1+\tilde y_2-x_2)=c'(x_2)\Rightarrow w_1+\tilde y_2-x_2=x_2\Rightarrow \;\boxed{x_2=\tfrac{w_1+\tilde y_2}{2}}.$
  2. INDX (with $x_1=0,q_1=0$): $\;-c(x_2)-c(w_1+\tilde y_2-x_2)+v_1=0$, and $w_1+\tilde y_2-x_2=x_2$, so $\;\boxed{v_1=2\,c\!\big(\tfrac{w_1+\tilde y_2}{2}\big)}.$
  3. $y_2$ stays interior, pinned by the P2 pair FOCY$_2$ & INDY$_2$ ($x_1=0$ collapses the P2 gap to $w_2-\tilde y_2$): $\;p_1c'(w_2-\tilde y_2)=c'(\tilde y_2-f),\ \ p_1\!\big(v_2-c(w_2-\tilde y_2)\big)=c(\tilde y_2-f).$

The common value here is $u_1=U_1(0)=0$: this stratum is also the full-dissipation locus $\{u_1=0\}$.

$f$-caveat (lower stratum). $x_1=0$ lies on this wall only when the comb floor $\tilde y_1=f\le 0$ (P1 favored / symmetric). For $f>0$ (P2 favored) the binding floor is $x_1=\tilde y_1=f$, and FOCX$_1$ there gives $q_1=c'(f)/c'(w_1)>0$ — so $\{x_1=0\}\!\Leftrightarrow\!\{q_1=0\}$ is the $f=0$ specialization. Faces 1–2 are written at this floor-at-$0$ representative.

Verdict. A genuine parameter surface $v_1=2c\!\big(\tfrac{w_1+\tilde y_2}{2}\big)$ — this is $\partial R_{2,2}$, the $\sigma(2,2)\!\to\!\sigma(2,1)$ transition where P2's lower atom dies. Empty if $c'(0)>0$. The comb node $\tilde y_2$ carries the second-round atom directly (the old "$+y_2$" in the reach gap).

2 · Its corner: $\;p_1=0$ cascade → corner

P1 drops its lower atom; everything below collapses.

  1. FOCY$_2$: $\;p_1c'(w_2+x_1-\tilde y_2)=c'(\tilde y_2-f)\Rightarrow 0=c'(\tilde y_2-f)\Rightarrow \;\tilde y_2=f\ \;(y_2=0).$
  2. Closure squeeze: $\;\tilde y_1\le x_1\le \tilde y_2=f$, and $\tilde y_1=f$, so $\;x_1=f\;(=0$ at $f{=}0)$  (the comb floor — forced by the domain, not by $F$).
  3. By §1 (at the $f{=}0$ representative), $x_1=0\Rightarrow \;q_1=0.$  So $\;p_1=0\ \Rightarrow\ y_2=0\ \Rightarrow\ x_1=0\ \Rightarrow\ q_1=0$  (both P2 atoms merge at $0$).

At the corner ($x_1{=}y_2{=}0,\ p_1{=}0,\ q_1{=}0$): P1 a single atom at $x_2$, P2 a single atom at $0$ — a $\sigma(1,1)$ point. Here $\tilde y_2=\tilde y_1=f$, so both P1-vs-P2 gaps coincide at $w_1+f-x_2$, and

  1. FOCX$_2$: $\;c'(w_1+f-x_2)=c'(x_2)\Rightarrow \;\boxed{x_2=\tfrac{w_1+f}{2}}$  ($=\tfrac{w_2-\Delta}{2}$).
  2. INDY$_2$: $\;0=c(0)=0$ ✓    INDX: $\;\boxed{v_1=2\,c\!\big(\tfrac{w_1+f}{2}\big)}.$
Verdict. The $y_2\to 0$ corner of the §1 surface — nested inside it, not independent. Empty if $c'(0)>0$.

3 · The full collapse: $\;q_1=1$  ($q_2=0$) collapse → σ(1,1)

P2 drops its upper atom; P1 collapses with it.

  1. FOCX$_1$: $\;c'(w_1+\tilde y_1-x_1)=c'(x_1)\Rightarrow w_1+\tilde y_1-x_1=x_1\Rightarrow \;x_1=\tfrac{w_1+\tilde y_1}{2}.$
  2. FOCX$_2$ ($q_2=0$): $\;c'(w_1+\tilde y_1-x_2)=c'(x_2)\Rightarrow w_1+\tilde y_1-x_2=x_2\Rightarrow \;x_2=\tfrac{w_1+\tilde y_1}{2}.$
  3. Hence $\;x_1=x_2=\tfrac{w_1+\tilde y_1}{2}$ — but the order needs $x_1Contradiction: P1's two atoms merge.

So $q_1=1$ forces both players to a single atom: P1 at $\tfrac{w_1+\tilde y_1}{2}=\tfrac{w_2-\Delta}{2}$, P2 at $0$ — the symmetric competitive $\sigma(1,1)$ equilibrium. INDX degenerates to $0=0$ (no extra condition; it is the $\sigma(1,1)$ existence point).

Verdict. The deep $\partial R_{2,2}$ corner — opposite extreme to §1. The whole atomic structure dissolves into one atom each.

4 · P1 to a single atom: $\;p_1=1$  ($p_2=0$) regime surface

P1 keeps only $x_1$; P2 still has two atoms — the $\sigma(1,2)$ edge.

  1. FOCY$_2$: $\;c'(w_2+x_1-\tilde y_2)=c'(\tilde y_2-f)\Rightarrow w_2+x_1-\tilde y_2=\tilde y_2-f\Rightarrow \;\boxed{\tilde y_2=\tfrac{w_2+x_1+f}{2}}\ \big(y_2=\tfrac{w_1+\Delta+x_1}{2}\big).$
  2. INDY$_2$: $\;v_2-c(w_2+x_1-\tilde y_2)=c(\tilde y_2-f)$, and $w_2+x_1-\tilde y_2=\tilde y_2-f$, so $\;\boxed{v_2=2\,c(\tilde y_2-f)=2\,c\!\big(\tfrac{w_1+\Delta+x_1}{2}\big)}.$
  3. $x_2$ carries mass $p_2=0$ (phantom); INDX survives as the boundary indifference $U_1(x_1)=U_1(x_2)$ — P1 just indifferent to re-opening a top atom.

No effort collapses — the binding comb bound $x_1\le\tilde y_2=\tfrac{w_2+x_1+f}{2}$ reduces to $x_1\le w_2+f$, comfortably slack. This is a genuine $\sigma(1,2)$ regime boundary $\partial(R_{2,2},R_{1,2})$.

Verdict & mirror. A P2-side condition $v_2=2c\!\big(\tfrac{w_1+\Delta+x_1}{2}\big)$ that mirrors §1's P1-side $v_1=2c\!\big(\tfrac{w_1+\tilde y_2}{2}\big)$ under the player swap $1\!\leftrightarrow\!2,\ \Delta\!\leftrightarrow\!-\Delta$ — the same exchange that runs through the swap-symmetry of the family.

5 · Comb wall $\;x_1=\tilde y_2$ cascade → corner

P1's lower atom rises to the win-tie with P2's upper atom — forcing $y_2\to0$.

This is the upper comb node binding ($x_1\le\tilde y_2$) — the $\tilde y$-correct wall of $\mathcal D$, replacing the old raw "$y_2=x_1$" (a wall only at $f=0$; for $f>0$ that lies strictly inside the comb). At $x_1=\tilde y_2$ the P2-winning gap opens to its maximum,

$$t_{1,2}=w_2+x_1-\tilde y_2=w_2\qquad\Longrightarrow\qquad v_2-c(t_{1,2})=v_2-c(w_2)=0$$

since $c(w_2)=v_2$ by definition of $w_2=c^{-1}(v_2)$. Clash $(1,2)$ is P2's only win, so its net value vanishing collapses P2's indifference:

  1. INDY$_2$: $\;-c(\tilde y_2-f)+p_1\underbrace{\big(v_2-c(w_2)\big)}_{=\,0}=0\;\Rightarrow\;c(\tilde y_2-f)=0\;\Rightarrow\;\tilde y_2=f\ \ (y_2=0).$
  2. Then $\tilde y_2=f=\tilde y_1$, and the squeeze $\tilde y_1\le x_1\le\tilde y_2$ pins $x_1=f$ — P2's upper atom has died.

Equivalently: any $\sigma(2,2)$ with a live $y_2>0$ keeps the comb gap strictly open, $x_1<\tilde y_2$. The wall is reachable only by killing $y_2$.

Verdict. Not a soft surface — $\{x_1=\tilde y_2\}$ cascades to $\{y_2=0\}$ (P2's upper atom dies), dropping out of $R_{2,2}$ into $\sigma(2,1)$. It carries no interior $\sigma(2,2)$; the contradiction with $F(z)=0$ is the strictly-open gap $x_1<\tilde y_2$.

6 · Comb wall $\;x_2=\tilde y_2$ support edge

P1's upper atom rises to the win-tie with P2's upper atom.

The top comb node binding ($\tilde y_2\le x_2$) — the $\tilde y$-correct wall, replacing the old raw "$x_2=y_2$". At $x_2=\tilde y_2$ clash $(2,2)$ ties, $s_{2,2}=w_1+\tilde y_2-x_2=w_1$, so $v_1-c(s_{2,2})=v_1-c(w_1)=0$. But this is only one of P1's three wins, so nothing collapses:

  1. FOCX$_2$: $\;q_1\,c'(w_1+\tilde y_1-\tilde y_2)+q_2\,c'(w_1)=c'(\tilde y_2).$
  2. FOCY$_2$: $\;p_1\,c'(w_2+x_1-\tilde y_2)=c'(\tilde y_2-f).$

No mass or effort is driven to a boundary; imposing $x_2=\tilde y_2$ over-determines $F$ by one equation — a codimension-1 surface in $\theta$.

Verdict. The genuine top-of-support edge of $R_{2,2}$, a soft regime surface (unlike §5: P1's other two wins keep the atom alive, no cascade). Touched only on a measure-zero $\theta$-surface — the cell sweep finds $x_2-\tilde y_2$ open throughout.

The lattice, not seven walls

Stacking the implications, the boundary collapses to a short chain. The lower faces — now including the upper comb node $\{x_1=\tilde y_2\}$ — cascade into one corner; only $\{p_1=1\}$ and the soft edge $\{x_2=\tilde y_2\}$ stand alone.

$$ \underbrace{\{p_1=0\}\ \text{ and }\ \{x_1=\tilde y_2\}}_{\text{both cascade}}\ \Longrightarrow\ \{y_2=0\}\ \Longrightarrow\ \underbrace{\{x_1=\tilde y_1\}\ \underset{f=0}{\Longleftrightarrow}\ \{q_1=0\}}_{\text{lower corner}} \qquad \underbrace{\{q_1=1\}}_{\Rightarrow\,x_1=x_2}\ \Longrightarrow\ \sigma(1,1) $$

independent regime surfaces:  $\{p_1=1\}\to\sigma(1,2)$  and the lone soft edge  $\{x_2=\tilde y_2\}$.

What this buys

Every face is a piece of $\partial R_{2,2}$ — a regime boundary where the $\sigma(2,2)$ configuration hands off to a neighbour ($\sigma(2,1)$, $\sigma(1,2)$, or $\sigma(1,1)$) or two atoms merge. None forces an $F$-zero for $\theta$ in the interior of $R_{2,2}$: the mass-collapse faces and the comb node $\{x_1=\tilde y_2\}$ cascade to corners (P2's upper atom dies); the lone soft edge $\{x_2=\tilde y_2\}$ needs a scalar $\theta$-relation. So, off those surfaces,

$F\neq 0$ on $\partial\mathcal D$ throughout $\operatorname{int}R_{2,2}$

— which is exactly the boundary-non-escape (properness) input the degree / global-uniqueness argument needs. The faces' job is not to be excluded one-by-one; it is to certify that the only way to touch $\partial\mathcal D$ is to leave $R_{2,2}$ through one of its named regime surfaces. The remaining quantitative obligation is the same for all seven: confirm those surfaces are the actual region boundary and do not cut $\operatorname{int}R_{2,2}$ — for which the cell sweep above is the evidence.

Method throughout: $c'(s)=c'(t)\Rightarrow s=t$ and $c(s)=c(t)\Rightarrow s=t$ (strict convexity), plus the closure squeeze on the comb $\tilde y_1\le x_1\le\tilde y_2\le x_2$ (with $\tilde y_1=f$, i.e. $y_1=0$). Reach gaps assumed positive where used; $c'(0)=0$ assumed for the cost family $e^r/r$, $r>1$ — drop it and the three mass-collapse faces are empty.
