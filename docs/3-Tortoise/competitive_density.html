Competitive equilibrium density and CDF

Symmetric two-player contest, last-round equilibrium. Parameters: $\Delta = 0$, $v_1 = v_2 = 1$, $c(x) = x^2$.
Reference: Joffrion & Parreiras, A Theory of Dynamic Contests (v10), Proposition 1 and Example (quadratic costs).

Formulas

From the proof of Proposition comp, the equilibrium CDF satisfies a first-order linear ODE whose general solution has the form $$ G(x) \;=\; (-1)^{\theta-1} + \sum_{k=1}^{\theta-1} C_k\,\exp(c_k\,x), $$ where the $c_k$ are roots of the characteristic polynomial $\,p(Z) = \sum_{k=0}^{\theta-1}(-1)^{\theta-1-k}\frac{\theta!}{k!}\,Z^{k}.$

For $\theta=2$, $v=1$: $\theta-1 = 1$, the characteristic polynomial has the single root $c_1 = 1$, and the boundary conditions $G(0)=0$, $G(\bar x)=1$ pin down $C_1=1$ and the upper endpoint $\bar x = \ln 2$.

CDF $$ G(x) \;=\; \begin{cases} 0 & x \le 0,\\[2pt] e^{x} - 1 & 0 \le x \le \ln 2,\\[2pt] 1 & x \ge \ln 2. \end{cases} $$

Density $$ g(x) \;=\; G'(x) \;=\; \begin{cases} e^{x} & 0 \le x \le \ln 2,\\[2pt] 0 & \text{otherwise}. \end{cases} $$
