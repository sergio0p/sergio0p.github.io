Continuation-Payoff Uniqueness — Status & To-Do

Tied-leaders final round, strictly convex costs, head starts. What is proved, what is numerically established (and how), and the exact lemmas still owed for a formal proof.
The claim to prove

In every Nash equilibrium of the 3-player final round with tied leaders (r1 = r2 = r > r3), strictly convex cost c and head starts hi, continuation payoffs are

u = (0, 0, v3K),  K = maxs (φ1φ2 − φ3)+

where φi(s) = c(s − hi)/vi (zero below the floor hi). In the floor regime (h3 > max(h1, h2), K > 0) the laggard earns a strictly positive rent — the value function is discontinuous on the tie line. Payoff uniqueness is what backward induction needs; equilibrium structure is provably non-unique and is not the target.

0 · Why K is the natural object

Player 3 bidding score s wins only if both leaders end below s, so his deviation payoff against CDFs F1, F2 is v3F1(s)F2(s) − c(s − h3) = v3[F1F2 − φ3](s). If the leaders were forced to randomize with F1F2 = φ1φ2 — which their own zero-rent indifference nearly does — player 3's best deviation would earn exactly v3·max g where g = φ1φ2 − φ3. g is the crux; its maximizer is t*. In the BKV world (no head starts) g ≤ 0 automatically and the classical exclusion gives u3 = 0; a high floor h3 frees the sign, and everything below is about showing u3 = v3K is then forced.

1 · What we already have — and how we got it

Proved R1. Leaders earn zero: u1 = u2 = 0 in any NE

How: the BKV (1996) support-transport argument survives the generalization verbatim up to the exclusion step — two players tied at the top reach compete each other's rent away; no linearity, no genericity needed. Documented in Docs/bkv_replication_tied_leaders.md. This is the launch pad: every argument below may assume it.

Proved R2. The floor rent exists: equilibria with u3 = v3K > 0

How: explicit construction (parks at own floors / three-way mix on [t*-side band] / pair region up to r), verified to 1e-9. This is where BKV's Lemma 8 exclusion forks: the conjecture u3 = 0 is false in the floor regime. Existence gives the lower half of uniqueness for free: the equilibrium payoff correspondence contains (0, 0, v3K); the proof must show it contains nothing else.

Numerical R3. Payoff uniqueness across all candidate structures — 189/189

How — the descent-tree march (descent_tree_march.jl, Sergio's design): anchor at the proved top pair {1,2} just below r, scan the score downward, and branch at every event — E1 (an inactive player's temptation ledger hits zero: entry), E2 (a player exits to a floor park), E3 (the closed-form CDF goes non-monotone: structural break). Regime labels are outputs, never inputs. Every root→leaf path is a candidate equilibrium; a leaf counts only if it passes an independent verifier (verify_strong: CDF validity on a grid hitting every floor/reach/atom ± ε, best response including just-above-atom probes, and every atom paying exactly ui). Across 63 quadratic + 63 cubic + 63 near-linear (θ = 9/8) floor-regime configs: every game has ≥ 1 genuine leaf, all genuine leaves agree, and the payoff is always (0, 0, v3K). Zero violations.

Numerical R4. Structure is NOT unique — so the proof target must be the payoff

How: the march finds ≥ 2 genuine leaves in every floor-regime game — the swap structures (one leader exits, player 3 takes over against the other); the symmetric knife-edge keeps a third (the pure three-way). The naïve "pause the dipping leader" resolution fails incentive compatibility (the paused leader strictly re-enters). Why it matters: any proof strategy that tries to force the support structure is chasing a false statement. The proof must be BKV-style — pin payoffs from indifference + feasibility, let structure fork. This redirect is itself a product of the numerics.

Numerical R5. The admissibility oracle — feasibility as a closed-form sign condition

How (admissible.jl, the complete-information image of Turocy's admissible-active-set test): on an interval where active set A (|A| = k) co-randomizes, multiplicative indifference gives closed-form CDFs, and monotonicity of Fi is equivalent to a density condition:

Di(s) = 1⁄(k−1) Σm∈A φ′m/φ̃m − φ′i/φ̃i  =  d log Fi/ds  ≥  0

(φ̃i = φi + ui/vi; frozen players drop out automatically.) Plus range Fi ∈ [0,1]. Validated 19/19 (finite-difference identity checks, machine-exact k = 2 case) and 51/51 against the march — two fully independent codepaths agreeing on every segment, boundary, and break. Why it matters for the proof: it converts "which active sets can an NE use where" into a finite, checkable vocabulary — the case-split criteria for a proof by exhaustion. Two pieces are already lemma-shaped: (i) for k = 2 the own hazard cancels analytically, so pairs are always admissible above both floors; (ii) D3 = 0 exactly at the argmax of g (hazards equalize where (φ1φ2)′ = φ′3 and φ1φ2 = φ3 + K) — a three-line computation.

Numerical R6. The two-sided squeeze on u3 — falsifications that are secretly the proof

How: feed the oracle wrong rents. With u3 = 0.8·v3K: F3 = √(φ1φ2/φ̃3) > 1 on the whole band — a range violation, the candidate is infeasible. With u3 = 1.2·v3K: pointwise fine, but F3 tops out at 0.915 — the support cannot close, a gluing failure invisible to any pointwise check. Too low ⇒ infeasible; too high ⇒ can't glue. That two-sided squeeze is the payoff-pinning argument (§2 writes it as mathematics).

Numerical R7. Continuity into the linear limit (θ = 9/8 stress)

How: at θ = 9/8 the three-way band collapses like t* − h3 = (κv3/θ)1/(θ−1) = (·)8 — down to 2.3e-15 on the sweep grid. The verified structure degenerates to the BKV linear-limit shape (P3 a pure atom at his floor) while u3 = v3K stays order-one and exact. The oracle resolved a 2.7e-7 band matching the closed form to 3.4e-6 relative. Why it matters: the rent is not a convexity artifact, and a formal proof must not assume an interior argmax — the boundary case t* → h3 is real.

Numerical R8. A generic second D3 zero above t* — a live case for T3

How (sentinel finding, 2026-07-06): a multi-peak sentinel added to the sweep driver fired on 123/189 configs; inspection showed one identical, benign shape everywhere — exactly two D3 zeros: the argmax t* (down-crossing, F3 local max) plus one up-crossing above it, the interior minimum of F32 = φ1φ2/φ̃3. It exists because the entrant's hazard φ′3/φ̃3 is hump-shaped — zero at his floor (by c′(0) = 0), rising, then decaying like θ/s — while the leaders' hazard sum decays slowly and eventually wins again. The crux g itself stayed single-peaked in all 189 configs. Why it matters: above t* a three-way mix becomes density-feasible again at the up-crossing. So the uniqueness case analysis (T3) cannot dismiss a restarted three-way region on density grounds — it must be killed by range or gluing (F3 sits below 1 with nowhere to close: the u3·1.2 mechanism of R6). A case we would likely have missed without the sentinel firing "wrongly."

2 · The pinning argument — the proof payload, written out

Assume R1 (u1 = u2 = 0). On any interval where both leaders are active, their indifference conditions read v1F2F3 = c(s−h1) and v2F1F3 = c(s−h2), i.e. F2F3 = φ1, F1F3 = φ2. Multiply and divide:

F1F2 = φ1φ2 / F32

Lower bound. Since F3 ≤ 1, F1F2 ≥ φ1φ2 on that region. If the region contains t*, player 3 deviating there secures v3(F1F2 − φ3)(t*) ≥ v3g(t*) = v3K. Hence u3 ≥ v3K. (This is R6's "0.8 rent is infeasible" made formal.)

Upper bound. At the top T3 of player 3's support, if F3(T3) = 1 and both leaders are active there, then F1F2 = φ1φ2 at T3, so u3 = v3g(T3) ≤ v3K. (R6's "1.2 rent can't close its support.")

The argument is one page conditional on three support facts: the interval structure itself (T1), the leaders' common region reaching t* (T3), and clean closure at T3 (T4). Those are the to-do.

3 · The to-do ledger — what a formal proof still owes

Open · Hard T1. Transport lemma (BKV L5/L7/L10 analog)

In any NE, supports decompose into finitely many maximal intervals with constant active sets; atoms only at floors (plus possibly the entrant's win-atom at closure); no gaps-with-mass-above pathologies. This is the measure-theoretic step numerics cannot supply — the one place we must write classical mathematics. Technology is standard (overbid and gap arguments transported from BKV's discussion-paper proofs; note the published BKV omits them — CentER DP 9051 has them), but every step must be re-checked against floors, which is exactly where L8 broke.

Ready to draft T2. Top anchor (Prop R)

Just below the tied top reach r, the active set is {1,2} with F3 = 1. Established during the replication work; needs a self-contained write-up. Combined with the oracle's k = 2 lemma (pairs always admissible), it anchors every descent path.

Open · Key case analysis T3. The leaders' common region reaches t*

The lower bound needs both leaders active at (or arbitrarily near) the crux argmax. Route: exhaustion over the oracle's vocabulary — if a leader's support has a gap covering t*, the interval must carry some other admissible active set ({1,3}, {2,3}, singletons), and one shows none of these can glue upward into the anchored pair region while keeping u1 = u2 = 0 and F3 ≤ 1. The descent tree is precisely this case skeleton — the march's event catalog becomes the proof's case list. One case is now known to be live (R8): above t* the three-way becomes density-feasible again at the generic up-crossing, so a restarted three-way region must be excluded by range/gluing, not density. Must also cover the boundary-argmax limit t* → h3 (R7) and, in principle, multi-peaked g (T6).

Open · Standard T4. Closure at player 3's support top

No atom of F3 at T3 (else a leader profitably overbids it), and both leaders active there. Standard overbid technology; short once T1 exists.

Ready to draft T5. The pinning proposition

Section 2 above, written as a proposition with the two-sided bound. Essentially done — one page, conditional on T1 + T3 + T4. Drafting it first is recommended: it fixes exactly what the support lemmas must deliver, no more.

Sentinel wired · Proof side open T6. Multi-peaked crux g

If g had several local maxima, the three-way support could be disconnected — the one geometry that would stress both the march's single-descent logic and T3's argument. The sweep driver's sentinel (_march_sweep_theta.jl) watches for this: it classifies each D3-zero's crossing direction and flags only a second F3 maximum — a naïve root count fires generically on R8's benign up-crossing instead. Confirmed: the refined sentinel re-ran the full 189-grid with zero flags (and 0 violations, 0 march failures) — no genuine multi-peak g anywhere. The proof-side task: either show convex c + power geometry forces a single peak on the relevant window, or handle the multi-peak case in T3 (only the global argmax pins K).

Deferred T7. Regime C emptiness

(h3 ≤ max(h1,h2) but K > 0.) Out of hypothesis for the floor-regime proposition; needed only for the general tie-line value function. Empty by probe at θ = 1, 2, 3, not proven. An oracle-driven enumerator (~1 day) when the paper needs the general claim.

Suggested order: T5 first (it is the specification), then T2, T4, T3, T1 — from cheapest to hardest, each step narrowing what the transport lemma must actually deliver. The 189/189 sweep makes it very unlikely we are proving a false statement; it substitutes for none of T1–T4.
