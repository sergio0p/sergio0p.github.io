σ(2,2) in (w₁, w₂, Δ) coordinates

The full equilibrium system, its polynomialization via vᵢ = c(wᵢ), and the Newton-polytope reading — symmetric fiber vs. generic member.  Draft exploration, 2026-06-01.

0. Setup and conventions

Atoms (raw coordinates): P1 at \(x_1 < x_2\) with masses \(p,\,1-p\); P2 at \(y_1=0 < y_2\) with masses \(q,\,1-q\). WLOG labeling \(u_2^\*=0\), so \(y_1=0\) is P2's boundary atom (no FOC there). The six unknowns are

$$ x_1,\; x_2,\; y_2,\; p,\; q,\; u_1 . $$

Parametrize by the reaches. Take \((w_1,w_2,\Delta)\) as the free parameters and define \(v_1=c(w_1),\; v_2=c(w_2)\). No inverse \(c^{-1}\) appears anywhere, so for polynomial \(c\) the whole system is polynomial over \(\mathbb{Q}(w_1,w_2,\Delta)\) — and over \(\mathbb{Q}\) once \(w_1,w_2,\Delta\) are fixed rationals. The asymmetry parameter is \(f = w_2-w_1-\Delta\); the symmetric fiber is \(\{f=0\}\), i.e. the hyperplane \(w_2=w_1+\Delta\) (the canonical symmetric point being \(w_1=w_2,\ \Delta=0\)).

The four effort gaps that enter Lemma 1's interior branch (raw coordinates):

All four are affine in the unknowns; \(f=w_2-w_1-\Delta\) lives entirely inside the constants (e.g. \(w_1+\Delta = w_2-f\)). For brevity below write \(a_1=w_2{-}\Delta{-}x_1\), \(a_2=w_2{-}\Delta{-}x_2\), \(b_2=w_2{-}\Delta{+}y_2{-}x_2\), \(d=w_1{+}\Delta{+}x_1{-}y_2\).

1. The system (general convex c)

Stationarity (FOC, marginal — uses \(c'\)) and indifference (level — uses \(c\)), with the asymmetric win-sets (P1 sums \(\ell\le k\), P2 sums \(k<\ell\)):

F1 P1 FOC @ \(x_1\):  $$ q\,c'(a_1) = c'(x_1) $$
F2 P1 FOC @ \(x_2\):  $$ q\,c'(a_2) + (1-q)\,c'(b_2) = c'(x_2) $$
I1 P1 indiff @ \(x_1\):  $$ q\big[c(w_1)-c(a_1)\big] - c(x_1) = u_1 $$
I2 P1 indiff @ \(x_2\):  $$ q\big[c(w_1)-c(a_2)\big] + (1-q)\big[c(w_1)-c(b_2)\big] - c(x_2) = u_1 $$
F3 P2 FOC @ \(y_2\):  $$ p\,c'(d) = c'(y_2) $$
I3 P2 indiff @ \(y_2\):  $$ p\big[c(w_2)-c(d)\big] - c(y_2) = 0 $$

Six equations, six unknowns. \(v_1=c(w_1)\) and \(v_2=c(w_2)\) are already substituted. Note the value brackets have the form \(c(w_i)-c(w_i+\text{gap})\), so the leading power of \(w_i\) cancels — the win-value decomposition. (At \(y_1=0\): all pairs give \(U^T_2=0\), so \(u_2(0)=-c(0)=0\) automatically — no equation.)

2. The system (quadratic c(e)=e²)

With \(c(e)=e^2,\ c'(e)=2e\) (common factors of 2 dropped from the FOCs):

E1 $$ q\,a_1 = x_1 $$
E2 $$ q\,a_2 + (1-q)\,b_2 = x_2 \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad (w_2-\Delta) - 2x_2 + (1-q)\,y_2 = 0 $$
E4 $$ q\big(w_1^2 - a_1^2\big) - x_1^2 = u_1 $$
E5 $$ q\big(w_1^2 - a_2^2\big) + (1-q)\big(w_1^2 - b_2^2\big) - x_2^2 = u_1 $$
E3 $$ p\,d = y_2 $$
E6 $$ p\big(w_2^2 - d^2\big) - y_2^2 = 0 $$

Cross-check: at \(w_1=w_2=w,\ \Delta=0\) these are exactly the validated symmetric σ(2,2) system, whose elimination polynomial in \(u_1\) has degree \(m^2=4\) and factors \(f_1\cdot f_2\) with \(\deg f_1=1,\ \deg f_2=3\), \(u_1^\*=\tfrac{q_1^2}{q_1+1}\).

Structural reading

  • Multilinear in the masses & payoff. Every equation is degree \(\le 1\) in \(p\), in \(q\), and in \(u_1\). So the Newton polytopes are thin (segments) in those three directions. This is the same mass-linearity that killed supermodularity / Rosen DSC (the zero on the pseudo-Jacobian's mass-diagonal) — here it is a polytope-thinning asset that holds the mixed volume down.
  • FOC ↔ indifference = marginal ↔ level. Quadratic terms in efforts appear only in the indifference rows, and always paired with a mass (\(q x_1^2,\ q x_2^2,\ p x_1^2,\dots\)) — the level equations are one degree higher in efforts than the marginal ones.
  • \(u_1\) is free. It appears only in E4, E5, linearly. Subtract E4−E5 to eliminate it, dropping to 5 equations in \((x_1,x_2,y_2,p,q)\).
The decisive point. The parameters \(w_1,w_2,\Delta\) appear only as coefficients — they never change which monomials are present. So the symmetric fiber \(\{f=0\}\) and a generic asymmetric point share the identical Newton polytopes. Turning on asymmetry does not inflate the support; it only moves the coefficients to a generic location.
Correction to an earlier hand-wave. In the \(\tilde y\)-shifted coordinates I described the shift \(c(\tilde y_2 - f)=(\tilde y_2-f)^\theta\) as "filling in" P2's own-cost monomial, suggesting asymmetry enlarges the polytope. That fill-in is a coordinate artifact of the \(f\)-dependent change \(\tilde y = y+f\) (singular at \(f=0\)). In the native raw coordinates the support is manifestly \(f\)-independent: P2's own cost is just \(c(y_2)=y_2^2\), and \(f\) sits in the constants. The honest statement is sharper: symmetric is a non-generic point in coefficient space, not a smaller polytope.

4. Does the count jump off the symmetric fiber?

Because the support is fixed, Bernstein/BKK gives one mixed-volume number \(\mathrm{MV}\) = the generic count of torus solutions, an upper bound for every choice of \((w_1,w_2,\Delta)\). The genuine \((2,2)\) equilibrium has \(p,q\in(0,1)\) nonzero, so it lives in the torus and is counted. The symmetric count is the elimination degree \(\deg w(2,2)=4\). So:

\(\mathrm{MV} = 4\)  ⟹  flat family: the generic asymmetric system has the same number of (complex) solutions as the symmetric one; the symmetric \(f_1\cdot f_2\) factorization is then a non-generic reducibility event at \(f=0\) without a drop in count.

\(\mathrm{MV} > 4\)  ⟹  genuine degeneration: at \(f=0\) extra roots collide or escape to the toric boundary (some mass \(\to 0/1\)), so the symmetric point both factors and loses degree. Asymmetry then has strictly more equilibrium candidates.

This is the precise, computable form of "does the count jump off \(f=0\)." Either answer is informative: flatness would say the symmetric algebra is the generic algebra in disguise (good for transporting results to \(v_1\ne v_2,\ \Delta\ne0\)); a jump would localize exactly which structure is special to symmetry.

How to settle it

  • Mixed volume directly: HomotopyContinuation.jl → mixed_volume on the 6 supports above (or polymake), giving \(\mathrm{MV}\) with no root-finding.
  • Generic specialization: fix a random rational \((w_1,w_2,\Delta)\) off \(\{f=0\}\), run the existing msolve elimination to \(u_1\), and read the degree; compare to 4. (Watch the toric/boundary roots and the spurious-root domain filter \(\mathcal D\).)
  • θ-scan: repeat for \(c=e^3,e^4\) to see how \(\mathrm{MV}\) grows with cost degree — the "monomial minimizes the count" conjecture.

General polynomial cost. For \(c=e^\theta\) the same six equations hold with \(c'(e)=\theta e^{\theta-1}\); efforts rise to degree \(\theta\) (indiff) / \(\theta-1\) (FOC), masses stay linear, and the value brackets \(c(w_i)-c(w_i+\text{gap})\) still cancel the leading \(w_i^\theta\). A general degree-\(d\) polynomial cost (normalized \(c(1)=1\) to keep \(w=1\) rational) is equally polynomial; it fills the effort-direction supports toward the full degree-\(d\) simplex, which is where the BKK count — and the loss of the clean factorization — comes from.
