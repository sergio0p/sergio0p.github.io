det J at σ(2,2): the critical set $\{\det J = 0\}$ ỹ-COORDINATES

Variables: $z = (x_1,\, x_2,\, \tilde y_2,\, u_1,\, p_1,\, p_2,\, q_1,\, q_2)$ ; Equations: $F = (\mathrm{FOC}_{x_1}, \mathrm{FOC}_{x_2}, \mathrm{FOC}_{\tilde y_2}, \mathrm{IND}_{x_1}, \mathrm{IND}_{x_2}, \mathrm{IND}_{\tilde y_2}, \Sigma p - 1, \Sigma q - 1)$ ; $J = \partial F / \partial z$ is the $8\times 8$ Jacobian, evaluated under $\mathrm{FOC}=0$.
What this artifact does. It locates the critical set $\mathcal C = \{z : \det J(z) = 0\}$ — the set of points where the σ(2,2) equilibrium map fails to be a local diffeomorphism — rather than merely asserting a sign. This is the object the Sard / generic-regularity program (Theorem 1) actually needs: the equilibrium manifold $\{F=0\}$ is regular exactly where it avoids $\mathcal C$. Two changes from the previous version:
  • ỹ-coordinates. P2's atoms are the shifted comb atoms $\tilde y_\ell = y_\ell + f$, $f = w_2 - w_1 - \Delta$. The interleaving $\tilde y_1 < x_1 < \tilde y_2 < x_2$ is the correct one; the raw chain $y_1 < x_1 < y_2 < x_2$ holds only at $f = 0$.
  • Critical set, not sign. We pull out the factor of $\det J$ that never vanishes, so $\mathcal C$ is carried by a single bracket, and we characterise where that bracket can hit zero.

0. The ỹ-coordinate setup & corrected interleaving

The change of variables $\tilde y_\ell = y_\ell + f$, with $f = w_2 - w_1 - \Delta$, pushes the entire dynamic asymmetry ($\Delta$ and the value gap) into P2's own first-round cost. After the shift:
  • the comb is $f$-free: $\;\tilde y_1 < x_1 < \tilde y_2 < x_2$, with comb floor $\tilde y_1 = f$ (raw $y_1 = 0$);
  • every win/clinch margin is $f$-free: P1 clinches $\tilde y_\ell$ at effort $w_1 + \tilde y_\ell - x_k$, P2 clinches $x_k$ at effort $w_2 + x_k - \tilde y_\ell$;
  • $f$ survives only in P2's own cost $c(\tilde y_\ell - f)$ — its level (indifference) and its marginal (FOC) — and, through the floor $\tilde y_1 = f$, in the two clinch gaps against the floor.
Equivalently, P2 plays the symmetric comb game against the translated cost $\tilde c_2(\cdot) = c(\cdot - f)$.

Why the raw chain was wrong off symmetry. The branch points of Lemma 1 are $w_2 - w_1$ and $w_2$ for $U^T_1$ (and $-w_1$, $w_2 - w_1$ for $U^T_2$); the two players do not share interior cuts. Writing the support as $y_1 < x_1 < y_2 < x_2$ silently assumes those cuts coincide, which happens only at $f = 0$ ($v_1 = v_2,\ \Delta = 0$). In ỹ-coordinates the wall positions are the comb nodes $x_1 = \tilde y_1$, $x_1 = \tilde y_2$, $x_2 = \tilde y_2$ — not the physical merges $y_2 = x_1$, $x_2 = y_2$, which sit strictly inside the comb for $f > 0$.

1. The determinant and its never-vanishing factor

Building the $8\times 8$ system in ỹ-coordinates and reducing under $\mathrm{FOC}=0$ (Wolfram, generic strictly-convex $c$) gives the exact factorisation

$$ \boxed{\;\det J \;=\; \underbrace{\bigl(-H_{x_2}\bigr)}_{<\,0\ \text{strictly}}\;\cdot\;\bigl(\mathcal A + \mathcal B\bigr)\;} $$ with $$ \mathcal A \;=\; p_1\, q_2\, c'[s_1]\, c'[s_3]\, \kappa_t, \qquad \mathcal B \;=\; \underbrace{\bigl(c[s_1] - c[s_2] + c[s_3] - v_1\bigr)}_{=\;\beta}\,\bigl(c[t] - v_2\bigr)\,H_{x_1}\, H_{y_2}. $$ (Verified symbolically: $\det J - (-H_{x_2})(\mathcal A + \mathcal B) = 0$. $\mathcal A$, $\mathcal B$ are the terms previously written $\mathcal A_1$, $\mathcal A_2 = B\!\cdot\! C$.)
The first factor never vanishes. $H_{x_2} = q_1 c''[s_2] + c''[x_2] + q_2 c''[s_3]$ is a sum of strictly positive terms for any strictly convex $c$ and interior masses, so $-H_{x_2} < 0$ on the whole admissible region. It contributes no critical points. Therefore $$ \boxed{\;\mathcal C \;=\; \{\det J = 0\} \;=\; \{\,\mathcal A + \mathcal B = 0\,\}\;} $$ — the critical set is carried entirely by the second factor. Signing $\det J$ and locating $\mathcal C$ are the same question; this artifact answers the second, sharper one.

2. The critical set lives in a single subregion

Two of the three pieces of $\mathcal A + \mathcal B$ have a fixed sign; only the bracket $\beta$ is free.

$\mathcal A$ — strictly positive everywhere

$$\mathcal A = p_1\, q_2\, c'[s_1]\, c'[s_3]\, \kappa_t$$ $p_1, q_2 > 0$; $c'[s_1], c'[s_3] > 0$ (gaps positive, $c$ convex); $\kappa_t = c'[t]^2 + (v_2 - c[t])c''[t] > 0$ because IND$_{\tilde y_2}$ gives $v_2 - c[t] = c[y_2]/p_1 > 0$.

$\mathcal B$ — sign $= -\operatorname{sign}(\beta)$

$$\mathcal B = \beta\,\underbrace{(c[t] - v_2)}_{=\,-c[y_2]/p_1\,<\,0}\,\underbrace{H_{x_1}H_{y_2}}_{>\,0}$$ $$\Longrightarrow\quad \operatorname{sign}(\mathcal B) = -\operatorname{sign}(\beta).$$
Substituting $c[t]-v_2 = -c[y_2]/p_1$ and multiplying by $p_1 > 0$, the critical equation $\mathcal A + \mathcal B = 0$ becomes $$ \boxed{\; \underbrace{p_1^2\, q_2\, c'[s_1]\, c'[s_3]\, \kappa_t}_{\text{regularising term} \;>\;0} \;=\; \underbrace{c[y_2]\;\beta\;H_{x_1}\, H_{y_2}}_{\text{bracket term}} \;} $$ The left side is strictly positive, so the equation can hold only where $\beta > 0$. Consequently:
  • On $\{\beta \le 0\}$: $\mathcal B \ge 0$, so $\mathcal A + \mathcal B \ge \mathcal A > 0$ and $\det J < 0$ unconditionally — $\mathcal C$ is empty here.
  • $\mathcal C \subseteq \{\beta > 0\}$: every critical point lies in the bracket-positive half, on the hypersurface where the regularising term and the bracket term exactly balance.

3. The bracket $\beta$ and the $f$-rewriting

The bracket $\beta = c[s_1] - c[s_2] + c[s_3] - v_1$ is the only sign-indeterminate ingredient. Two reductions tame it.

IND substitution eliminates $v_1$

Using $\mathrm{IND}_{x_2} - \mathrm{IND}_{x_1}$ to remove $v_1$ (and $q_1 + q_2 = 1$): $$ \boxed{\; q_2\, \beta \;=\; \bigl(c[s_1] - c[s_2]\bigr) \;-\; \bigl(c[x_2] - c[x_1]\bigr) \;} $$ a cross-difference of cost values: both $c[s_1]-c[s_2]>0$ ($s_1>s_2$) and $c[x_2]-c[x_1]>0$ ($x_2>x_1$), of the same first order $\approx c'(\cdot)(x_2-x_1)$ — hence no a-priori sign.

The $\varphi$-symmetry

Let $K := w_2 - \Delta = w_1 + f$ and $\varphi(x) := c(K - x) + c(x)$ — strictly convex, symmetric about $K/2$, minimised there. Since $s_i = K - x_i$, $$ q_2\, \beta \;=\; \bigl(c[s_1] + c[x_1]\bigr) - \bigl(c[s_2] + c[x_2]\bigr) \;=\; \varphi(x_1) - \varphi(x_2), $$ so $\operatorname{sign}(\beta) = \operatorname{sign}\!\bigl(\varphi(x_1) - \varphi(x_2)\bigr)$ — determined by which atom is farther from $K/2$. On the computed equilibria the atoms always straddle the midpoint, $x_1 < K/2 < x_2$ (401 of 401), whence $$ \boxed{\;\operatorname{sign}(\beta) \;=\; \operatorname{sign}(K - x_1 - x_2) \;=\; \operatorname{sign}(w_2 - \Delta - x_1 - x_2)\;} $$ The critical set therefore can only meet the equilibrium manifold where the atoms are inward of the midline, $x_1 + x_2 < w_2 - \Delta$.

4. Quadratic specialisation: $\mathcal C = \{T_1 = T_2\}$

For $c(e) = e^2/2$ the never-vanishing factor collapses to $-H_{x_2} = -2$, and after eliminating $v_1$ (from $\mathrm{IND}_{x_2}-\mathrm{IND}_{x_1}$) and $v_2$ (from $\mathrm{IND}_{\tilde y_2}$) the determinant is exactly $$ \boxed{\;\det J \;=\; \frac{T_1 - T_2}{p_1\, q_2}\;} \qquad \Longrightarrow\quad \mathcal C = \{T_1 = T_2\},\quad \operatorname{sign}(\det J) = \operatorname{sign}(T_1 - T_2), $$ with $$ T_1 = (x_2 - x_1)\,(w_2 - \Delta - x_1 - x_2)\, y_2^2\, (1 + q_1)(1 + p_1), \qquad T_2 = 2\, p_1\, q_2^2\, s_1\, s_3\,\Bigl(p_1\, t^2 + \tfrac12 y_2^2\Bigr). $$ (Verified symbolically: $\det J_{\text{quad,IND}} - (T_1 - T_2)/(p_1 q_2) = 0$.)

Both $T_1, T_2 > 0$ in the inward regime $x_1 + x_2 < w_2 - \Delta$ (this is the $\beta>0$ half again, since the second factor of $T_1$ is $K - x_1 - x_2$). The critical hypersurface is the polynomial equation $T_1 = T_2$:

$$ (x_2 - x_1)(w_2 - \Delta - x_1 - x_2)\, y_2^2\, (1+q_1)(1+p_1) \;=\; 2\, p_1\, q_2^2\, s_1\, s_3\,\bigl(p_1\, t^2 + \tfrac12 y_2^2\bigr). $$ $\det J < 0$ (the regular side) is the strict inequality $T_1 < T_2$.
Both identities are on-manifold. $\det J = (-H_{x_2})(\mathcal A + \mathcal B)$ and the quadratic $\det J = (T_1-T_2)/(p_1 q_2)$ hold once the stationarity relations are imposed on the determinant — the first-order conditions $c'(x_k) = $ (mass-weighted $c'$ at the gaps) and the indifference relations that fix $v_1, v_2$. A symbolic determinant compared without substituting these does not match: it carries extra off-manifold terms that cancel only at a stationary point. The clean factorisation is a statement about the Jacobian at an equilibrium — exactly what regularity needs. Verified in Wolfram with all relations imposed (residuals identically $0$, both signs reconciled); a naïve off-manifold symbolic comparison reports a spurious mismatch.
Scale-invariance. Along the equilibrium manifold $v_1, v_2$ scale with the cost (they are pinned by indifference, $v_2 = c[t] + c[y_2]/p_1$, etc.), so $\mathcal A$ and $\mathcal B\!\cdot\!\mathcal C$ are homogeneous of the same degree under $c \mapsto \lambda c$. The critical locus $\{\mathcal A + \mathcal B\!\cdot\!\mathcal C = 0\} = \{T_1 = T_2\}$ is therefore unchanged by an overall rescaling of $c$ — the sweep's pure-form costs ($e^2, e^3, \dots$) carry no special normalisation, and the $\det J < 0$ conclusion is not an artifact of the cost convention.

5. How close does the equilibrium manifold come to $\mathcal C$?

Define the dimensionless signed margin $\;m := \dfrac{\mathcal A + \mathcal B}{\mathcal A} = 1 + \dfrac{\mathcal B}{\mathcal A}$. The critical set is exactly $\{m = 0\}$; $\det J < 0 \iff m > 0$. Because $\operatorname{sign}(\mathcal B) = -\operatorname{sign}(\beta)$: on the threatening side $\beta > 0$ the term $\mathcal B$ pulls $m$ toward $0$ ($m = 1 - |\mathcal B|/\mathcal A$); on the reinforcing side $\beta < 0$ it pushes $m$ away ($m = 1 + |\mathcal B|/\mathcal A > 1$).

6. The Sard / generic-regularity reading

$\mathcal C = \{\mathcal A + \mathcal B = 0\}$ is a codimension-one semialgebraic hypersurface in the $(x_1, x_2, \tilde y_2, p, q)$ space (for each parameter $(w_1, w_2, \Delta)$). Theorem 1 (generic regularity) asks that the equilibrium manifold $\{F = 0\}$ avoid $\mathcal C$ for a.e. parameter — equivalently that $0$ be a regular value of $F$, so that $\det J \ne 0$ along $\{F=0\}$. What this analysis contributes:
  • $\mathcal C$ is confined: it cannot meet the equilibrium manifold anywhere in the half $\{\beta \le 0\} = \{x_1 + x_2 \ge w_2 - \Delta\}$. Only the inward half can host a critical point.
  • On the inward half, $\mathcal C$ is the balance hypersurface $p_1^2 q_2\, c'[s_1] c'[s_3] \kappa_t = c[y_2]\,\beta\, H_{x_1} H_{y_2}$ — a single scalar equation whose two sides are explicit and positive.
  • Numerically the manifold clears $\mathcal C$ on every tested cell ($\det J < 0$, 0 sign flips over 13 cost families) — strong evidence that $0$ is a regular value there. But the margin is family-dependent: $m \ge 0.831$ holds only for the original four families; on near-linear powers $e^{1.2}, e^{1.3}$ it drops to $m \approx 0.33$. So this is not yet a proof over the full $(w_1, w_2, \Delta)$ region, and in particular gives no uniform lower bound on $m$ (the uniform-properness / boundary-non-escape obligation, Theorem 3).

7. What we have and what we don't

Have

  • Exact factorisation $\det J = (-H_{x_2})(\mathcal A + \mathcal B)$ in ỹ-coordinates, generic convex $c$.
  • Never-vanishing factor $-H_{x_2} < 0$ removed $\Rightarrow$ $\mathcal C = \{\mathcal A + \mathcal B = 0\}$.
  • $\mathcal A > 0$ always; $\operatorname{sign}(\mathcal B) = -\operatorname{sign}(\beta)$ $\Rightarrow$ $\mathcal C \subseteq \{\beta > 0\}$, and $\det J < 0$ unconditionally on $\{\beta \le 0\}$.
  • $q_2\beta = \varphi(x_1) - \varphi(x_2)$; on the data $\operatorname{sign}(\beta) = \operatorname{sign}(w_2 - \Delta - x_1 - x_2)$.
  • Quadratic closed form $\det J = (T_1 - T_2)/(p_1 q_2)$; explicit polynomial $\mathcal C = \{T_1 = T_2\}$, and $\{\beta > 0,\text{ quadratic}\}$ proved ($\det J < 0$) via TARGET-with-equality ($c'''=0$).
  • $\det J < 0$ empirically robust: 0 sign flips on 13 cost families, admissibility-gated (target_counterexample_hunt.jl).
  • Exact on-manifold grouping identity $p_1(\mathcal A+\mathcal B) = q_2 c'[s_1]c'[s_3](c'[y_2]^2 + p_1 c[y_2]c''[t]) - \beta H_{x_1} c[y_2](p_1 c''[t]+c''[y_2])$.

Don't have

  • A proof that $\det J < 0$ on $\{\beta > 0\}$ for general convex $c$ — empirically true (0 flips) but the secant $\to$ TARGET $\to D_1\ge 0$ chain is too lossy ($D_1 < 0$ on 27 genuine admissible equilibria, $e^{1.1}$–$e^{1.3}$, where $\det J<0$ still holds). The secant bound breaks for $c''' < 0$.
  • Any uniform lower bound on the margin $m = (\mathcal A+\mathcal B)/\mathcal A$: it is family-dependent and drops to $0.33$ ($e^{1.2}, e^{1.3}$). The $\le 0.169$ threat-side ratio is four-families-only.
  • The uniform-properness / boundary-non-escape lemma (Theorem 3) that would upgrade "regular on the computed box" to "regular generically over all parameters".
  • Generalisation of $\mathcal C$'s structure to σ$(m,n)$ beyond $(2,2)$.
